# Dei Verbum

## DOGMATIC CONSTITUTION ON DIVINE REVELATION

**Solemnly Promulgated by His Holiness Pope Paul VI on November 18, 1965**

---

## PREFACE

**1.** "Hearing the word of God with reverence and proclaiming it with faith, the sacred synod takes its direction from these words of St. John" about eternal life and fellowship with the Father and Son. Following the Council of Trent and First Vatican Council, this council establishes authentic doctrine on divine revelation and its transmission, so "by hearing the message of salvation the whole world may believe, by believing it may hope, and by hoping it may love."

---

## CHAPTER I: REVELATION ITSELF

**2.** God chose to reveal Himself and His will's hidden purpose through Christ, the Word made flesh, so humanity might access the Father through the Holy Spirit and share in divine nature. Through revelation, the invisible God speaks to people as friends and lives among them, inviting fellowship. "This plan of revelation is realized by deeds and words having an inner unity" where divine actions manifest teaching while words clarify the mystery. Christ is both revelation's mediator and fullness.

**3.** God gives humanity enduring witness through creation. Planning salvation's way, He manifested Himself to our first parents. After their fall, His redemption promise aroused hope. He continuously cared for humanity, then called Abraham to form a great nation. Through patriarchs, Moses, and prophets, He taught Israel to know Him as the living, true, provident, and just God, and to await the promised Savior, preparing the Gospel across centuries.

**4.** After speaking variously through prophets, "God has spoken to us in His Son." He sent the eternal Word enlightening all people to dwell among them and reveal God's innermost being. Jesus Christ, the Word made flesh, was "a man to men." He speaks God's words and completed His Father's salvation work. "To see Jesus is to see His Father." Jesus perfected revelation through His presence and manifestation via words, deeds, signs, wonders, death, glorious resurrection, and Spirit-sending. He confirmed that God liberates from sin's darkness, raising us to eternal life.

The Christian dispensation, as the new and definitive covenant, will never pass away. We await no further public revelation before Christ's glorious manifestation.

**5.** "The obedience of faith" must be given to God who reveals—an obedience where humans freely commit themselves entirely, offering intellect and will's full submission, freely assenting to revealed truth. God's grace and the Holy Spirit's interior help must precede and assist, moving hearts toward God, opening minds' eyes, and giving joy in assenting to truth. The Holy Spirit constantly brings faith to completion by His gifts for deeper revelation understanding.

**6.** Through divine revelation, God chose to show and communicate Himself and His eternal will's decisions regarding human salvation—sharing divine treasures totally transcending human understanding. God, all things' beginning and end, can be known with certainty from created reality by human reason's light; but He teaches that religious truths naturally accessible to reason can be known by all people "with ease, with solid certitude and with no trace of error, even in this present state of the human race."

---

## CHAPTER II: HANDING ON DIVINE REVELATION

**7.** God graciously ensured that revelation for all nations' salvation would abide perpetually in full integrity and be handed to all generations. Christ the Lord, in whom supreme God's full revelation is completed, commissioned the Apostles to preach the Gospel—salvation's source and all moral teaching—imparting heavenly gifts. This Gospel was promised through prophets and fulfilled and promulgated by Christ. The Apostles faithfully fulfilled this commission through oral preaching, example, and observances, handing on what they received from Christ's lips, living with Him, His deeds, and Holy Spirit's prompting. Apostles and apostolic men committed the salvation message to writing under the same Holy Spirit's inspiration.

To keep the Gospel forever whole and alive within the Church, the Apostles left bishops as successors, "handing over" teaching authority "in their own place." This sacred tradition and Sacred Scripture of both Old and New Testaments are like a mirror where the pilgrim Church on earth looks at God, from whom she received everything, until finally seeing Him face to face.

**8.** Apostolic preaching, expressed specially in inspired books, was preserved by unending preacher succession until time's end. Therefore, the Apostles, handing on what they received, warn the faithful to hold fast to traditions learned by word or letter, and to defend the faith handed on once and for all. What the Apostles handed on includes everything contributing toward God's people's holiness and faith increase; so the Church, in teaching, life and worship, perpetuates and hands on to all generations all she is and believes.

This tradition from the Apostles develops in the Church with the Holy Spirit's help. There is growth in understanding handed-down realities and words through believers' contemplation and study, who treasure these things in their hearts, through penetrating spiritual understanding from experience, and through preaching by those receiving through Episcopal succession truth's sure gift. As centuries succeed one another, the Church constantly moves toward divine truth's fullness until God's words reach complete fulfillment in her.

The holy fathers' words witness to this living tradition's presence, whose wealth pours into the believing, praying Church's practice and life. Through this tradition, the Church's full sacred books' canon is known, and sacred writings are more profoundly understood and unceasingly made active in her; thus God, who spoke long ago, uninterruptedly converses with His beloved Son's bride; and the Holy Spirit, through whom the Gospel's living voice resounds in the Church and world, leads believers unto all truth and makes Christ's word dwell abundantly in them.

**9.** Close connection and communication exists between sacred tradition and Sacred Scripture. Both flowing from the same divine wellspring merge into a certain unity and tend toward the same end. Sacred Scripture is God's word consigned to writing under the divine Spirit's inspiration, while sacred tradition takes the word entrusted by Christ the Lord and Holy Spirit to the Apostles, handing it on to successors in full purity, so that led by truth's Spirit light, they proclaim it, preserve it faithfully, explain it, and make it more widely known. Consequently, the Church's certainty about revealed things doesn't come from Sacred Scripture alone. Therefore, both sacred tradition and Sacred Scripture must be accepted and venerated with the same loyalty and reverence sense.

**10.** Sacred tradition and Sacred Scripture form one sacred deposit of God's word committed to the Church. Holding fast to this deposit, the entire holy people united with their shepherds remain always steadfast in the Apostles' teaching, common life, bread-breaking and prayers, so that holding to, practicing and professing faith's heritage becomes the bishops' and faithful's single common effort.

But the word of God's authentic interpretation task, whether written or handed on, has been entrusted exclusively to the Church's living teaching office, whose authority is exercised in Jesus Christ's name. This teaching office is not above God's word but serves it, teaching only what has been handed on, listening to it devoutly, guarding it scrupulously and explaining it faithfully in accord with a divine commission and the Holy Spirit's help. It draws from this one faith deposit everything it presents for belief as divinely revealed.

It is clear that sacred tradition, Sacred Scripture and the Church's teaching authority, in accord with God's most wise design, are so linked and joined that one cannot stand without the others, and all together and each in its own way under the one Holy Spirit's action contribute effectively to souls' salvation.

---

## CHAPTER III: SACRED SCRIPTURE, ITS DIVINE INSPIRATION AND INTERPRETATION

**11.** Divinely revealed realities contained and presented in Sacred Scripture have been committed to writing under the Holy Spirit's inspiration. Holy mother Church, relying on the Apostles' belief, holds that the Old and New Testaments' books in their entirety, with all their parts, are sacred and canonical because written under the Holy Spirit's inspiration—they have God as author and have been handed on as such to the Church herself. In composing sacred books, God chose men and while employed by Him they made use of their powers and abilities, so that with Him acting in and through them, they, as true authors, consigned to writing everything and only those things He wanted.

Therefore, since everything asserted by inspired authors or sacred writers must be held as asserted by the Holy Spirit, Scripture books must be acknowledged as teaching solidly, faithfully and without error that truth God wanted in sacred writings for salvation's sake. Therefore "all Scripture is divinely inspired and has its use for teaching the truth and refuting error, for reformation of manners and discipline in right living, so that the man who belongs to God may be efficient and equipped for good work of every kind."

**12.** However, since God speaks in Sacred Scripture through humans in human fashion, the Sacred Scripture interpreter, to see clearly what God wanted to communicate, should carefully investigate what meaning the sacred writers really intended, and what God wanted to manifest by their words.

To search out the sacred writers' intention, attention should be given, among other things, to "literary forms." Truth is set forth and expressed differently in texts which are variously historical, prophetic, poetic, or other discourse forms. The interpreter must investigate what meaning the sacred writer intended to express and actually expressed in particular circumstances by using contemporary literary forms in accordance with his time and culture's situation. For correct understanding of what the sacred author wanted to assert, customary and characteristic styles of feeling, speaking and narrating prevailing at the sacred writer's time, and patterns normally employed in everyday dealings, deserve due attention.

But, since Holy Scripture must be read and interpreted in the sacred spirit in which it was written, no less serious attention must be given to the whole Scripture's content and unity if sacred texts' meaning is to be correctly worked out. The whole Church's living tradition must be taken into account along with faith's elements' harmony. It is exegetes' task to work according to these rules toward better understanding and explanation of Sacred Scripture's meaning, so that through preparatory study the Church's judgment may mature. For all said about Scripture interpretation is finally subject to the Church's judgment, which carries out God's divine commission and ministry of guarding and interpreting God's word.

**13.** In Sacred Scripture, while God's truth and holiness always remains intact, the marvelous "condescension" of eternal wisdom is clearly shown, "that we may learn the gentle kindness of God, which words cannot express, and how far He has gone in adapting His language with thoughtful concern for our weak human nature." God's words, expressed in human language, have been made like human discourse, just as the eternal Father's Word, when He took human weakness's flesh, was in every way made like humans.

---

## CHAPTER IV: THE OLD TESTAMENT

**14.** In carefully planning and preparing all humanity's salvation, the infinite love God, by special dispensation, chose a people for Himself to whom He would entrust His promises. First He entered into covenant with Abraham and, through Moses, with Israel's people. To this people He acquired for Himself, He so manifested Himself through words and deeds as the one true, living God that Israel came to know by experience God's ways with humans. When God Himself spoke through prophets' mouths, Israel daily gained deeper, clearer understanding of His ways and made them more widely known among nations. The plan of salvation foretold by sacred authors, recounted and explained by them, is found as God's true word in Old Testament books: these books, therefore, written under divine inspiration, remain permanently valuable. "For all that was written for our instruction, so that by steadfastness and the Scriptures' encouragement we might have hope."

**15.** The old covenant plan's principal purpose was to prepare for Christ's coming, all's redeemer and the messianic kingdom, to announce this coming by prophecy, and to indicate its meaning through various types. Now the Old Testament books, in accordance with salvation's state established by Christ before mankind's time, reveal to all people God and humanity's knowledge and the ways in which God, just and merciful, deals with humans. These books, though containing some incomplete and temporary things, nevertheless show us true divine pedagogy. These same books then express a lively God sense, contain sublime teachings about God, sound human life wisdom, and a wonderful prayer treasury, and in them our salvation's mystery is present in a hidden way. Christians should receive them with reverence.

**16.** God, both Testaments' inspirer and author, wisely arranged that the New Testament be hidden in the Old and the Old be made manifest in the New. For, though Christ established the new covenant in His blood, still the Old Testament books with all their parts, caught up into the Gospel's proclamation, acquire and show forth their full meaning in the New Testament and in turn shed light on it and explain it.

---

## CHAPTER V: THE NEW TESTAMENT

**17.** God's word, which is salvation power for all who believe, is set forth and shows its power most excellently in New Testament writings. For when time's fullness arrived, the Word was made flesh and dwelt among us in His graces and truth's fullness. Christ established God's kingdom on earth, manifested His Father and Himself by deeds and words, and completed His work by His death, resurrection and glorious Ascension and by sending the Holy Spirit. Having been lifted up from the earth, He draws all people to Himself—He who alone has eternal life's words. This mystery had not been manifested to other generations as it was now revealed to His holy Apostles and prophets in the Holy Spirit, so that they might preach the Gospel, stir up faith in Jesus Christ and Lord, and gather together the Church. Now the New Testament writings stand as a perpetual and divine witness to these realities.

**18.** It is common knowledge that among all Scriptures, even New Testament ones, the Gospels have special preeminence, and rightly so, for they are the incarnate Word, our savior's life and teaching's principal witness.

The Church has always and everywhere held and continues to hold that the four Gospels are of apostolic origin. For what the Apostles preached in fulfilling Christ's commission, afterwards they themselves and apostolic men, under the divine Spirit's inspiration, handed on to us in writing: faith's foundation, namely, the fourfold Gospel, according to Matthew, Mark, Luke and John.

**19.** Holy Mother Church has firmly and with absolute constancy held, and continues to hold, that the four Gospels just named, whose historical character the Church unhesitatingly asserts, faithfully hand on what Jesus Christ, while living among humans, really did and taught for their eternal salvation until the day He was taken up into heaven. Indeed, after the Lord's Ascension the Apostles handed on to their hearers what He had said and done. This they did with that clearer understanding which they enjoyed after they had been instructed by Christ's life's glorious events and taught by the truth's Spirit light. The sacred authors wrote the four Gospels, selecting some things from many which had been handed on by word or writing, reducing some to a synthesis, explaining some things in view of their churches' situation and preserving the proclamation form but always in such fashion that they told us the honest truth about Jesus. For their writing intention was that either from their own memory and recollections, or from those who "themselves from the beginning were eyewitnesses and ministers of the Word" witness we might know "the truth" concerning matters about which we have been instructed.

**20.** Besides the four Gospels, the New Testament canon also contains St. Paul's epistles and other apostolic writings, composed under the Holy Spirit's inspiration, by which, according to God's wise plan, matters concerning Christ the Lord are confirmed, His true teaching is more and more fully stated, the divine work of Christ's saving power is preached, the Church's story and its marvelous growth are told, and its glorious fulfillment is foretold.

For the Lord Jesus was with His apostles as He had promised and sent them the advocate Spirit who would lead them into truth's fullness.

---

## CHAPTER VI: SACRED SCRIPTURE IN THE LIFE OF THE CHURCH

**21.** The Church has always venerated divine Scriptures just as she venerates the Lord's body, since, especially in sacred liturgy, she unceasingly receives and offers to the faithful life's bread from God's word table and Christ's body. She has always maintained them, and continues to do so, together with sacred tradition, as faith's supreme rule, since, as God-inspired and committed once and for all to writing, they impart God's word Himself without change, and make the Holy Spirit's voice resound in the prophets' and Apostles' words. Therefore, like the Christian religion itself, all Church preaching must be nourished and regulated by Sacred Scripture. For in sacred books, the Father who is in heaven meets His children with great love and speaks with them; and God's word's force and power is so great that it stands as the Church's support and energy, her sons' faith strength, the soul's food, the pure and everlasting spiritual life's source. Consequently these words apply perfectly to Sacred Scripture: "For the word of God is living and active" and "it has power to build you up and give you your heritage among all those who are sanctified."

**22.** Easy access to Sacred Scripture should be provided for all Christian faithful. That is why the Church from the very beginning accepted as her own that very ancient Greek translation of the Old Testament called the Septuagint; and she has always given other Eastern translations and especially Latin ones, the Latin translation known as the Vulgate, honor's place. But since God's word should be accessible at all times, the Church by her authority and with maternal concern sees to it that suitable and correct translations are made into different languages, especially from the sacred books' original texts. And should the opportunity arise and Church authorities approve, if these translations are produced in cooperation with the separated brethren as well, all Christians will be able to use them.

**23.** The incarnate Word's bride, the Church taught by the Holy Spirit, is concerned to move ahead toward deeper Sacred Scripture understanding so that she may increasingly feed her sons with divine words. Therefore, she also encourages both East and West's holy Fathers' study and sacred liturgies' study. Catholic exegetes then and other sacred theology students, working diligently together and using appropriate means, should devote their energies, under the Church's sacred teaching office's watchful care, to divine writings' exploration and exposition. This should be so done that as many divine word ministers as possible will be able to effectively provide the God's people Scripture nourishment, enlighten their minds, strengthen their wills, and set humans' hearts on fire with God's love. The sacred synod encourages the Church's sons and Biblical scholars to continue energetically, following the Church's mind, with the work they have so well begun, with constant vigor renewal.

**24.** Sacred theology rests on the written God's word, together with sacred tradition, as its primary and perpetual foundation. By scrutinizing in faith's light all truth stored up in Christ's mystery, theology is most powerfully strengthened and constantly rejuvenated by that word. For the Sacred Scriptures contain God's word and since they are inspired, really are God's word; and so the sacred page's study is, as it were, sacred theology's soul. By the same Scripture word the word ministry also, that is, pastoral preaching, catechetics and all Christian instruction, in which the liturgical homily must hold the foremost place, is nourished in a healthy way and flourishes in a holy way.

**25.** Therefore, all clergy must hold fast to the Sacred Scriptures through diligent sacred reading and careful study, especially Christ's priests and others, such as deacons and catechists who are legitimately active in the word ministry. This is to be done so that none of them will become "an empty preacher of the word of God outwardly, who is not a listener to it inwardly" since they must share the abundant divine word wealth with the faithful committed to them, especially in sacred liturgy. The sacred synod also earnestly and especially urges all Christian faithful, especially Religious, to learn by frequent divine Scripture reading the "excellent Jesus Christ knowledge." "For ignorance of the Scriptures is ignorance of Christ." Therefore, they should gladly put themselves in touch with the sacred text itself, whether it be through the liturgy, rich in divine word, or through devotional reading, or through suitable purpose instructions and other aids which, in our time, with Church shepherds' approval and active support, are commendably spread everywhere. And let them remember that prayer should accompany Sacred Scripture reading, so that God and humans may talk together; for "we speak to Him when we pray; we hear Him when we read the divine saying."

It devolves on sacred bishops "who have the apostolic teaching" to give the faithful entrusted to them suitable instruction in the divine books' right use, especially the New Testament and above all the Gospels. This can be done through sacred texts translations, which are to be provided with the necessary and really adequate explanations so that the Church's children may safely and profitably become conversant with the Sacred Scriptures and be penetrated with their spirit.

Furthermore, editions of the Sacred Scriptures, provided with suitable footnotes, should be prepared also for the non-Christians' use and adapted to their situation. Both souls' pastors and Christians generally should see to these' wise distribution in one way or another.

**26.** In this way, therefore, through sacred books' reading and study "the word of God may spread rapidly and be glorified" and the treasure of revelation, entrusted to the Church, may more and more fill humans' hearts. Just as the Church's life is strengthened through more frequent Eucharistic mystery celebration, similarly we may hope for a new stimulus for the Spirit's life from growing reverence for God's word, which "lasts forever."
