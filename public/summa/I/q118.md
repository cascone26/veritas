# Question 118: The Production of Man from Man as to the Soul

## Article 1: Whether the Sensitive Soul is Transmitted with the Semen

### Objection 1

A perfect substance not composed of matter and form that begins to exist acquires existence through creation. The sensitive soul is a perfect substance. Therefore it begins through creation, not generation.

### Objection 2

The generating power belongs to the vegetative soul, which is lower than the sensitive soul. Nothing acts beyond its species. Therefore the sensitive soul cannot be caused by generating power.

### Objection 3

The form of the generator must be actually present in the cause of generation. Neither the sensitive soul nor any part of it is in the semen.

### Objection 4

If some principle in the semen produces the sensitive soul, either it remains or it does not—both options lead to difficulties.

### On the contrary

"Let the waters bring forth the creeping creatures having life" (Genesis 1:20). The power in semen relates to seminally generated animals as elemental power relates to those produced by putrefaction.

### I answer that

If the sensitive soul were subsistent with its own being and operation, it would be created. However, the sensitive soul's being and operation belong to the composite; it ceases to exist when the body perishes. Since being and operation belong to composites, both the sensitive soul and similar forms are brought into existence by corporeal agents reducing matter from potentiality to act.

The semen derives active force from the soul of the generator, as an instrument derives power from the principal agent. Whether the generated soul is caused by the generator's soul or by seminal power derived from it makes no difference.

### Reply to Objection 1

The sensitive soul is not a perfect self-subsistent substance.

### Reply to Objection 2

The generating power begets by virtue of the whole soul. The more perfect the soul, the more perfect the effect.

### Reply to Objection 3

The active force in semen is a certain movement of the generator's soul, not the soul itself except virtually. This force is based on the vital spirit in semen, containing heat from heavenly bodies. Hence "man and the sun generate man."

### Reply to Objection 4

The male's seminal power transmutes matter until informed by the sensitive soul. Once produced, the seminal force ceases—instrumental movement ceases once the effect is produced.

## Article 2: Whether the Intellectual Soul is Produced from the Semen

### Objection 1

Genesis 46:26 states: "All the souls that came out of Jacob's thigh, sixty-six." Therefore the intellectual soul is produced from semen.

### Objection 2

The intellectual, sensitive, and nutritive souls are substantially one in man. The sensitive soul is generated from semen. Therefore the intellectual soul is also.

### Objection 3

One agent acts upon both matter and form. The body is produced by seminal power, so the intellectual soul should be too.

### Objection 4

Man begets his like in species, constituted by the rational soul. Therefore the rational soul comes from the begetter.

### Objection 5

If created by God, sometimes God would concur in adultery's sin by creating souls from illicit intercourse.

### On the contrary

The Ecclesiastical Dogmas state: "The rational soul is not engendered by coition."

### I answer that

An active power in matter cannot produce an immaterial effect. The intellectual principle transcends matter, having operations independent of the body. Therefore seminal power cannot produce the intellectual principle. Aristotle says: "The intellect alone comes from without."

Since the intellectual soul has an operation independent of the body, it is subsistent. As an immaterial substance, it can only be caused through creation by God. To hold otherwise is to hold the soul non-subsistent and perishing with the body. Therefore it is heretical to say the intellectual soul is transmitted with semen.

We must say that when a more perfect form supervenes, the previous form is corrupted, yet the supervening form contains the previous form's perfection. Through many generations and corruptions we arrive at the ultimate substantial form in man. The intellectual soul is created by God at human generation's end, and this soul is simultaneously sensitive and nutritive, with pre-existing forms corrupted.

### Reply to Objection 1

Part is substituted for whole—soul for the whole man—by synecdoche.

### Reply to Objection 2

The same soul initially vegetative becomes sensitive through seminal power's action, but the intellectual soul cannot be a perfection of the pre-existing soul without either being a separate subsistent substance (returning to multiple souls) or being non-subsistent (perishing with the body). The correct view: the intellectual soul is created by God, simultaneously being sensitive and nutritive.

### Reply to Objection 3

Where many agents are ordered to one another, the higher agent's power reaches the ultimate form while inferior agents only dispose matter. Seminal power disposes matter; the intellectual soul comes from God alone.

### Reply to Objection 4

Man begets his like insofar as seminal power disposes matter for receiving a certain form species.

### Reply to Objection 5

In the adulterer's action, what is natural is good; God concurs in this. What is inordinate lust is evil; God does not concur in this.

## Article 3: Whether Human Souls Were Created Together at the Beginning of the World

### Objection 1

Genesis 2:2 states God rested from all His work. Creating new souls daily would contradict this.

### Objection 2

If souls were created with bodies, innumerable spiritual substances would daily be added, making the universe imperfect at the beginning.

### Objection 3

The intellectual soul remains when the body perishes. Therefore it began before the body.

### On the contrary

The Ecclesiastical Dogmas state: "The soul is created together with the body."

### I answer that

Some maintained that union to the body is accidental to the intellectual soul, so souls were created with angels at the beginning. This is false. If union were accidental, man resulting from this union would be accidental, or the soul itself would be man. The human soul differs in nature from angels by its different mode of understanding through senses and phantasms. The soul needs bodily union.

If it is natural to the soul to be united to the body, it is unnatural to be without it. God should not begin His work with imperfect and unnatural things. If union is not natural but voluntary, there would be no reason why souls created from the beginning should later wish bodily union; and this body's union with this soul would seem chance-dependent. If union results from violent cause, the soul's embodiment would be penal—Origen's condemned opinion.

Since all these opinions are unreasonable, souls were not created before bodies but are created simultaneously as they are infused into them.

### Reply to Objection 1

God rested from creating new genera and species. Souls created now existed already regarding species-likeness in the first works.

### Reply to Objection 2

Something can be added daily regarding number of individuals, not regarding number of species.

### Reply to Objection 3

The soul's remaining without the body results from sin's corruption. God should not make the soul without the body from the beginning.
