# Question 112: The Mission of the Angels

## Article 1: Whether the Angels Are Sent on Works of Ministry

### Objection 1

Every mission requires a determinate place. Since intellectual actions abstract from "here" and "now," angels cannot be sent to perform their own actions.

### Objection 2

The empyrean heaven befits angelic dignity. If angels are sent to minister, their dignity would be diminished.

### Objection 3

External occupation hinders contemplation. If angels are sent on external ministrations, their beatitude would be lessened.

### Objection 4

To minister belongs to inferiors. Angels are naturally greater than humans, so they should not be sent to administer to us.

### On the contrary

Scripture states: "Behold I will send My angels who shall go before thee" (Exodus 23:20).

### I answer that

Angels are sent in ministry by God. An angel is "here" but not "there" because its power reaches some things but not others. When an angel must perform work concerning corporeal creatures, it applies itself anew to that body by divine command.

The angel's action proceeds from God as first principle and is ordered to God as final end. Therefore, angels' actions are called "ministries" and they are said to be sent in ministry.

### Reply to Objection 1

An operation regulated and commanded by intellect can have determinate place, even though contemplation dwelling in intellect itself requires no place.

### Reply to Objection 2

An angel loses no dignity when absent from the empyrean, as a king loses no dignity when not seated upon his throne.

### Reply to Objection 3

Angels regulate external actions by intellect alone, so external occupations assist rather than hinder contemplation. Gregory notes: "The angels do not go abroad as to lose the delights of inward contemplation."

### Reply to Objection 4

Angels minister chiefly to God and secondarily to us—not because we are superior, but because anyone cleaving to God becomes one spirit with God and thereby superior to every creature.

## Article 2: Whether All Angels Are Sent in Ministry

### Objection 1

The Apostle states: "All are ministering spirits, sent to minister" (Hebrews 1:14).

### Objection 2

A Seraph was sent to purify the prophet's lips (Isaiah 6:6-7). Therefore inferior orders are certainly sent.

### Objection 3

The Divine Persons infinitely excel all angelic orders, yet they are sent. Therefore all angels are sent.

### Objection 4

If superior angels are not sent, only the last angel would be sent, contradicting Daniel 7:10.

### On the contrary

Gregory, citing Dionysius, states: "The higher ranks fulfil no exterior service."

### I answer that

Some held superior angels are generally not sent but sometimes are by divine dispensation. However, the angelic order follows grace's gifts, and suspending angelic order would provide no perceptible benefit. Therefore, following Dionysius, superior angels are never sent to external ministry.

### Reply to Objection 1

As divine missions include visible and invisible missions, so angelic missions include external missions (not all angels sent) and interior missions (all angels sent, as one enlightens another).

### Reply to Objection 2

According to Dionysius, the angel purifying the prophet's lips belonged to an inferior order but was called "Seraph" equivocally because he came to kindle the prophet's lips.

### Reply to Objection 3

Divine Persons are not sent in ministry but called "sent" equivocally.

### Reply to Objection 4

Multiple grades exist in divine ministries. Superior sent angels handle higher ministries while lower ones handle inferior ministries.

## Article 3: Whether All Sent Angels Assist

### Objection 1

Gregory states: "The angels are sent, and assist; for though the angelic spirit is limited, yet God is not limited."

### Objection 2

Raphael said: "I am one of the seven who stand before the Lord" (Tobit 12:15). Therefore sent angels assist.

### Objection 3

Holy angels are nearer to God than Satan. Yet Satan was present among the sons of God (Job 1:6).

### Objection 4

If inferior angels do not assist, only the highest angel would assist, contradicting Daniel 7:10.

### On the contrary

Gregory states: "Those powers assist, who do not go forth as messengers to men."

### I answer that

All angels immediately gaze upon the Divine Essence—in this respect all, even ministering angels, assist. Yet not all perceive divine mystery's secrets in the Divine Essence's clearness, but only superior angels. Regarding this perception, only the highest hierarchy's superior angels are said to assist, being immediately enlightened by God.

### Reply to Objection 1

Gregory explains the first mode of assisting: sent angels can always assist and see the Father's face.

### Reply to Objection 2

Based on the first mode of assisting.

### Reply to Objection 3

Satan is described as present among assistants, not as assisting. Gregory notes he retained a nature like the angels though he lost beatitude.

### Reply to Objection 4

All assistants see some things immediately in divine glory. Higher members see more, some enlightening others.

## Article 4: Whether All Angels of the Second Hierarchy Are Sent

### Objection 1

The second hierarchy's angels do not assist. Therefore all must be sent in ministry.

### Objection 2

Gregory states more angels minister than assist. This would not hold if second hierarchy angels were not sent.

### On the contrary

Dionysius states the "Dominations are above all subjection." Since being sent implies subjection, dominations are not sent.

### I answer that

Being sent to external ministry properly belongs to angels acting by divine command regarding corporeal creatures. Angels of orders whose names signify administration are sent: Angels, Archangels, Virtues, Powers, and Principalities. The four superior orders are not sent.

### Reply to Objection 1

Dominations are reckoned among ministering angels not as executing but as disposing and commanding others' actions.

### Reply to Objection 2

Gregory explains that ministers' number is indefinite while assistants' number, though expressed finitely, signifies vastly more assistants than ministers.
