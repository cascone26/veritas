# Question 93: The End or Term of the Production of Man

## Article 1: Whether the Image of God Is in Man

### Objection 1
Isaiah 40:18 asks, "To whom have you likened God?" suggesting no image of God exists in man.

### Objection 2
The Son alone is "the image of the invisible God" (Colossians 1:15).

### Objection 3
No common species exists between God and man, nor equality. Therefore, no image exists.

### On the contrary
"Let Us make man to Our own image and likeness" (Genesis 1:26).

### I answer that
Augustine distinguishes likeness from image: likeness is essential to image, but image adds that something is "copied from" another. Man possesses imperfect likeness to God, copied from God as exemplar, yet lacking equality since the exemplar infinitely exceeds the copy.

### Reply to Objection 1
The Prophet speaks of bodily images. God created a spiritual image in man.

### Reply to Objection 2
The Son is the perfect Image. Man is called "to the image" due to imperfect resemblance—like a king's image on a silver coin.

### Reply to Objection 3
A creature is "one" with God analogically, though not numerically or specifically.

## Article 2: Whether the Image of God Is in Irrational Creatures

### Objection 1
Effects are contingent images of their causes. God causes all creatures, so all possess His image.

### Objection 2
The solar ray has great similitude to Divine goodness.

### Objection 3
The universe exceeds man in goodness.

### Objection 4
Boethius says God forms the world "into His image."

### On the contrary
Augustine states only intellectual creatures bear God's image.

### I answer that
Not every likeness constitutes an image. Image requires specific likeness following the ultimate difference. Creatures resemble God through existence, life, and understanding. "Intellectual creatures alone are made to God's image," approaching God most nearly in likeness.

### Reply to Objection 1
Irrational creatures possess minimal image-nature through whatever likeness they have, yet fall short of true image.

### Reply to Objection 2
Dionysius compares the solar ray regarding causality, not natural dignity.

### Reply to Objection 3
The universe exceeds in extensive goodness, but intellectual nature contains superior intensive likeness.

### Reply to Objection 4
Boethius uses "image" to mean likeness between product and artist's mental species.

## Article 3: Whether Angels Are More to God's Image Than Man

### Objection 1
Augustine states God granted no creature besides man to be His image.

### Objection 2
No creature mediates between God and man.

### Objection 3
Substance admits no degree.

### On the contrary
Gregory states the angel is "seal of resemblance" with "greater expression."

### I answer that
Regarding intellectual nature (image's foundation), angels are more perfectly God's image. However, in certain accidental respects—man proceeding from man as God from God, soul pervading the whole body as God does the world—man more nearly resembles God. Absolutely speaking, angels are more to God's image.

### Reply to Objection 1
Augustine excludes irrational creatures, not angels.

### Reply to Objection 2
Man's soul is generically most akin to God while angels exceed specifically.

### Reply to Objection 3
One substance-species excels another. Angels' intellectual substance is more perfect.

## Article 4: Whether God's Image Is Found in Every Man

### Objection 1
1 Corinthians 11:7 says man is God's image, but woman is man's glory.

### Objection 2
Not all are predestined to God's image.

### Objection 3
Sin makes man unlike God, destroying the image.

### On the contrary
"Surely man passeth as an image" (Psalm 38:7).

### I answer that
Three ways image exists: natural aptitude for understanding and loving God (common to all), habitual knowledge and love through grace (in the just), and perfect knowledge and love (in the blessed). A threefold image: of creation, recreation, and likeness.

### Reply to Objection 1
Intellectual nature exists in both man and woman. Genesis 1:27 confirms both bear God's image.

### Reply to Objections 2 and 3
These concern image through grace and glory, not natural image.

## Article 5: Whether God's Image in Man Represents the Trinity of Persons

### Objection 1
Augustine and Hilary state image corresponds to the Divine Essence, not Person-distinction.

### Objection 2
Various Fathers attribute image to qualities belonging to Essence-unity.

### Objection 3
If man's image represented the Trinity, natural reason could know the Trinity.

### Objection 4
Only the Son is termed "image of the Father."

### On the contrary
Hilary states "the plurality of Divine Persons is proved from the fact that man was made to God's image."

### I answer that
Divine Person-distinction suits the Divine Nature. Therefore, imaging Divine Nature does not exclude imaging Divine Persons. Man's image exists both regarding Divine Nature and Trinity of Persons.

### Reply to Objection 3
We see our internal trinity but believe rather than see God's Trinity.

### Reply to Objection 4
Augustine notes the Father said "Let Us make man to Our image"—not "Thy image"—indicating the whole Trinity's action.

## Article 6: Whether God's Image Is Only in Man's Mind

### Objection 1
Man is not only mind.

### Objection 2
Male-female distinction in bodies suggests image includes bodily aspect.

### Objection 3
Image applies principally to shape, which belongs to body.

### Objection 4
Trinities exist in corporeal and imaginary vision too.

### On the contrary
Ephesians 4:23-24 attributes renewal and image to the mind.

### I answer that
The intellect is the rational creature's distinguishing feature; therefore, image resides in mind alone. Other parts exhibit trace-likeness. An "image" represents by species-likeness; a "trace" represents as effect without species-likeness.

### Reply to Objection 1
Man is "called" God's image because the image is impressed on his mind.

### Reply to Objection 2
Both sexes possess image in the mind, wherein sexual distinction vanishes.

### Reply to Objection 3
Man's upright posture represents the soul's image-bearing by trace, not by proper image.

### Reply to Objection 4
Corporeal and imaginary trinities imperfectly represent the Divine Trinity.

## Article 7: Whether God's Image Is in the Acts of the Soul

### Objection 1
Existing is not an act, yet Augustine mentions it in the image.

### Objection 2
Augustine assigns image to mind, knowledge, and love. Mind signifies essence, not act.

### Objection 3
Memory, understanding, and will are natural powers, not acts.

### Objection 4
The Trinity-image always remains, but acts do not always exist.

### On the contrary
Augustine assigns the soul's lower trinity to actual vision.

### I answer that
Divine Persons distinguish by word-procession and love-procession. "Word cannot exist without actual thought." Therefore, image primarily resides in soul-acts. Image secondarily exists in powers and habits as acts exist therein virtually.

### Reply to Objection 3
Memory, understanding, and will more preeminently bear the Trinity-image. "Since word requires actual thought," these chiefly constitute image.

### Reply to Objection 4
The soul always remembers, understands, and loves itself habitually. Acts exist always in principles.

## Article 8: Whether God's Image Is in the Soul Only by Comparing with God as Object

### Objection 1
Word and love-procession occur regarding any object.

### Objection 2
Augustine states image is sought in the whole soul.

### Objection 3
Meritorious God-knowledge requires grace, so image is not natural to all.

### Objection 4
Saints know temporal things in beatific vision.

### On the contrary
Augustine: image exists because the mind "can remember, understand, and love God."

### I answer that
Image requires species-representation. Diversity of objects diversifies word-species. Image relates to the verbal concept born from God-knowledge. Image is found as the soul turns toward God or possesses nature enabling God-turning.

### Reply to Objection 2
Temporal-knowledge habits lack permanence. Image is not seen in temporal knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 3
Natural God-knowledge and love exist. The mind naturally possesses reason-capacity for understanding God.

### Reply to Objection 4
Beatific vision sees temporal things in God Himself.

## Article 9: Whether "Likeness" Is Properly Distinguished from "Image"

### Objection 1
Likeness relates to image as genus to species.

### Objection 2
Immortality and indivisibility are not specific to image.

### Objection 3
Image is threefold: nature, grace, and glory.

### Objection 4
Knowledge and virtue-love both belong to image.

### On the contrary
Augustine asks why both terms were mentioned if identical.

### I answer that
Likeness precedes image as more general. Image denotes intellectual properties. Likeness regards more universal qualities (incorruptibility representing Divine immutability). Likeness also perfects image—measuring representation's perfection. Damascene: image implies "intelligent being with free-will"; likeness implies "power-likeness insofar as possible."

### Reply to Objection 1
Likeness is not generally distinct from image but insofar as it falls short of or perfects image-notion.

### Reply to Objection 2
The soul's essence belongs to image through intellectual-nature respects, not subsequent conditions.

### Reply to Objection 3
Natural virtues establish natural likeness. Image and likeness apply from different perspectives.

### Reply to Objection 4
Love of the word belongs to image-nature; virtue-love belongs to likeness.
