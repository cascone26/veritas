# Question 117: The Action of Man

## Article 1: Whether One Man Can Teach Another

### Objection 1

Scripture states "Be not you called Rabbi" (Matthew 22:8), with Jerome's gloss noting this honors God alone. Since teaching belongs to masters, man cannot teach.

### Objection 2

If one man teaches another by causing knowledge, then knowledge would be an active quality producing its like in another.

### Objection 3

Man cannot cause intellectual light or intelligible species in another, so man cannot cause knowledge through teaching.

### Objection 4

Teachers only propose signs. If signs represent known things, the disciple already knows; if unknown, signs convey nothing.

### On the contrary

The Apostle declares: "Whereunto I am appointed a preacher and an apostle...a doctor of the Gentiles" (1 Timothy 2:7).

### I answer that

The teacher reduces the learner from potentiality to actuality. Knowledge arises from both interior principle (personal research) and exterior principle (instruction). Every man possesses the active intellect's light, grasping universal principles. Through applying these to particular things, one advances from known to unknown.

The master leads the disciple from known to unknown in two ways. First, by proposing helps—less universal propositions or sensible examples—enabling the intellect to reach previously unknown truth. Second, by strengthening the learner's intellect through presenting the order of principles to conclusions. Thus "a demonstration is a syllogism that causes knowledge."

### Reply to Objection 1

The teacher provides exterior help like the physician. The interior intellect's light is the principal cause, both from God.

### Reply to Objection 2

Knowledge directs teaching but is not an active quality producing its like.

### Reply to Objection 3

The master moves the disciple so the latter's intellect forms intelligible concepts from externally proposed signs.

### Reply to Objection 4

Signs proposed represent things known generally and confusedly, leading to distinct and detailed knowledge.

## Article 2: Whether Man Can Teach Angels

### Objection 1

The Apostle states that "the manifold wisdom of God may be made known to the principalities and powers through the Church" (Ephesians 3:10).

### Objection 2

Some men receive immediate instruction from God's Word, particularly the apostles. Therefore some men can teach angels.

### Objection 3

Some men exceed certain angels in virtue. Therefore inferior angels can be instructed by men.

### On the contrary

Dionysius teaches that superior angels are never enlightened by inferior ones. Men rank below even the lowest angels (Matthew 11:11). Therefore angels are never enlightened by men regarding Divine things. Men may make their thoughts known to angels through speech, since only God knows heart's secrets.

### Reply to Objection 1

When Christ's mysteries were fulfilled through the apostles' preaching, certain mysteries became apparent to angels through the events themselves, not through human instruction.

### Reply to Objection 2

The apostles were instructed by God's Word according to His human nature, not Divinity.

### Reply to Objection 3

Certain men exceed certain angels virtually—possessing charity meriting higher beatitude—though actually much lesser, as a great tree's seed is virtually greater than a small tree.

## Article 3: Whether Man by the Power of His Soul Can Change Corporeal Matter

### Objection 1

Gregory states saints work miracles sometimes by their power, as Peter raised Tabitha. Therefore men change matter through soul power.

### Objection 2

Some bewitch others by a single look, especially children. This requires the soul's power changing corporeal matter.

### Objection 3

The soul's apprehension changes the body through heat and cold, sometimes causing sickness and death. Therefore the soul can change exterior matter.

### On the contrary

Augustine states: "Corporeal matter obeys God alone at will."

### I answer that

Corporeal matter receives form only through agents composed of matter and form, or through God. The soul cannot change corporeal matter by natural power except through bodies.

### Reply to Objection 1

Saints work miracles through grace's power, not nature's.

### Reply to Objection 2

A strong imagination changes the body's corporeal spirits, especially in the eyes. These taint surrounding air. When a soul is intensely moved toward wickedness, the countenance becomes venomous, especially harming children with tender bodies. Demons may cooperate.

### Reply to Objection 3

The soul as the body's form can change its own united body through the sensitive appetite. However, the soul's apprehension alone cannot change exterior bodies.

## Article 4: Whether the Separate Human Soul Can Move Bodies Locally

### Objection 1

A body naturally obeys a spiritual substance regarding local motion. The separate soul is spiritual. Therefore it can move exterior bodies.

### Objection 2

Simon Magus reportedly retained power over a slain child's soul for magical wonders.

### On the contrary

Aristotle states: "The soul cannot move any other body whatsoever but its own."

### I answer that

The separate soul cannot move a body by natural power. Even while united, the soul moves only its body as endowed with life. Since the soul naturally determines to moving its body's form, it cannot naturally move another body, though God's power can exceed these limits.

### Reply to Objection 1

If a separate substance's motive power naturally determines to a certain body, it cannot move other bodies. The soul naturally determines to its own body.

### Reply to Objection 2

Augustine and Chrysostom state demons often pretend to be dead souls. Simon Magus was likely deceived by a demon.
