# Question 61: The Production of the Angels in the Order of Natural Being

## Article 1: Whether the angels have a cause of their existence?

### Objection 1
Genesis treats of things created by God but makes no mention of angels. Therefore angels were not created by God.

### Objection 2
The Philosopher states that if any substance be a form without matter, "it has being and unity of itself, and has no cause of its being." Angels are immaterial forms, therefore they have no cause of their being.

### Objection 3
Whatever is produced by an agent receives form from it. Since angels are forms, they do not derive their form from any agent. Therefore angels have no active cause.

### On the contrary
Psalm 148:2 and 5 state: "Praise ye Him, all His angels" and "For He spoke and they were made."

### I answer that
Angels and everything existing except God were made by God. God alone is His own existence; in everything else, essence differs from existence. God alone exists of His own essence; all other things have their existence by participation. Whatever exists by participation is caused by what exists essentially, as everything ignited is caused by fire. Consequently angels were necessarily made by God.

### Reply to Objection 1
Augustine states that angels were not passed over in Genesis but are designated by "heavens" or "light." They were either passed over or designated by corporeal names because Moses addressed an uncultured people incapable of understanding incorporeal nature. Revealing incorporeal creatures would have inclined them toward idolatry.

### Reply to Objection 2
Substances that are subsisting forms have no formal cause of their existence and unity, nor an active cause producing effects by changing matter from potentiality to actuality. However, they have a cause productive of their entire substance.

### Reply to Objection 3
The solution to this difficulty is manifest from the reply to Objection 2.

## Article 2: Whether the angel was produced by God from eternity?

### Objection 1
God is the cause of the angel by His being, for He does not act through something besides His essence. God's being is eternal. Therefore He produced angels from eternity.

### Objection 2
Whatever exists at one period and not at another is subject to time. The angel is above time. Therefore the angel is not at one time existing and at another non-existing but exists always.

### Objection 3
Augustine proves the soul's incorruptibility by the fact that the mind is capable of truth. But as truth is incorruptible, so is it eternal. Therefore the intellectual nature of the soul and of the angel is not only incorruptible but likewise eternal.

### On the contrary
Proverbs 8:22, in the person of begotten Wisdom, states: "The Lord possessed me in the beginning of His ways, before He made anything from the beginning." Angels were made by God, therefore at one time they were not.

### I answer that
God alone, Father, Son and Holy Ghost, is from eternity. Catholic Faith holds this without doubt; everything to the contrary must be rejected as heretical. God produced creatures such that He made them "from nothing"; that is, after they had not been.

### Reply to Objection 1
God's being is His will. The fact that God produced angels by His being does not exclude that He made them by His will. God's will does not act by necessity in producing creatures. Therefore He produced such as He willed, and when He willed.

### Reply to Objection 2
An angel is above that time which measures the movement of the heavens, because he is above every movement of corporeal nature. Nevertheless he is not above time which measures the succession of his existence after non-existence and the succession in his operations. Augustine states that "God moves the spiritual creature according to time."

### Reply to Objection 3
Angels and intelligent souls are incorruptible by the very fact of having a nature whereby they are capable of truth. But they did not possess this nature from eternity; it was bestowed upon them when God willed it. Consequently it does not follow that angels existed from eternity.

## Article 3: Whether the angels were created before the corporeal world?

### Objection 1
Jerome states: "Six thousand years of our time have not yet elapsed; yet how shall we measure the time, how shall we count the ages, in which the Angels, Thrones, Dominations, and the other orders served God?" Damascene also reports that some say angels were begotten before all creation, as Gregory the Theologian declares.

### Objection 2
The angelic nature stands midway between the Divine and corporeal natures. The Divine nature is from eternity; corporeal nature is from time. Therefore the angelic nature was produced ere time was made, and after eternity.

### Objection 3
The angelic nature is more remote from corporeal nature than one corporeal nature is from another. Yet one corporeal nature was made before another, as shown by the six days of creation in Genesis. Much more, therefore, was the angelic nature made before every corporeal nature.

### On the contrary
Genesis 1:1 states: "In the beginning God created heaven and earth." This would not be true if anything had been created previously. Consequently angels were not created before corporeal nature.

### I answer that
A twofold opinion exists among the Fathers on this point. The more probable one holds that angels were created at the same time as corporeal creatures. Angels are part of the universe; they do not constitute a universe of themselves but unite with corporeal natures in constituting one universe. The mutual relationship of creatures makes up the good of the universe. No part is perfect if separate from the whole. Consequently it is improbable that God, Whose "works are perfect," should have created the angelic creature before other creatures. However, the contrary is not deemed erroneous, especially on account of the opinion of Gregory Nazianzen, whose authority in Christian doctrine carries great weight.

### Reply to Objection 1
Jerome speaks according to the teaching of the Greek Fathers, all of whom hold the creation of angels to have taken place previously to that of the corporeal world.

### Reply to Objection 2
God is not a part of but far above the whole universe, possessing within Himself the entire perfection of the universe in a more eminent way. An angel is a part of the universe. Hence the comparison does not hold.

### Reply to Objection 3
All corporeal creatures are one in matter; angels do not agree with them in matter. Consequently the creation of the matter of the corporeal creature involves in a manner the creation of all things; but the creation of angels does not involve creation of the universe. If the contrary view be held, then in Genesis 1:1, "In the beginning God created heaven and earth," the words "In the beginning" must be interpreted "In the Son" or "In the beginning of time," but not "In the beginning, before which there was nothing," unless we say "Before which there was nothing of the nature of corporeal creatures."

## Article 4: Whether the angels were created in the empyrean heaven?

### Objection 1
Angels are incorporeal substances. A substance which is incorporeal is not dependent upon a body for its existence; consequently, neither is it for its creation. Therefore angels were not created in any corporeal place.

### Objection 2
Augustine remarks that angels were created in the upper atmosphere; therefore not in the empyrean heaven.

### Objection 3
The empyrean heaven is said to be the highest heaven. If angels were created in the empyrean heaven, it would not beseem them to mount up to a still higher heaven. This is contrary to what is said in Isaiah, speaking in the person of the sinning angel: "I will ascend into heaven."

### On the contrary
Strabus, commenting on "In the beginning God created heaven and earth," states: "By heaven he does not mean the visible firmament, but the empyrean, that is, the fiery or intellectual firmament, which was filled with angels directly it was made."

### I answer that
The universe is made up of corporeal and spiritual creatures. Consequently spiritual creatures were created so as to bear some relationship to the corporeal creature, and to rule over every corporeal creature. Hence it was fitting for angels to be created in the highest corporeal place, as presiding over all corporeal nature; whether it be styled the empyrean heaven or whatever else it be called. Isidore states that the highest heaven is the heaven of the angels, explaining Deuteronomy 10:14: "Behold heaven is the Lord's thy God, and the heaven of heaven."

### Reply to Objection 1
Angels were created in a corporeal place not as depending upon a body either as to their existence or as to their being made; God could have created them before all corporeal creation, as many holy Doctors hold. They were made in a corporeal place in order to show their relationship to corporeal nature, and that they are by their power in touch with bodies.

### Reply to Objection 2
By the uppermost atmosphere Augustine possibly means the highest part of heaven, to which the atmosphere has a kind of affinity owing to its subtlety and transparency. Or else he is not speaking of all angels but only of such as sinned, who, in the opinion of some, belonged to the inferior orders. Nothing hinders saying that the higher angels, as having exalted and universal power over all corporeal things, were created in the highest place of the corporeal creature; while other angels, as having more restricted powers, were created among the inferior bodies.

### Reply to Objection 3
Isaiah is not speaking of any corporeal heaven but of the heaven of the Blessed Trinity; unto which the sinning angel wished to ascend when he desired to be equal in some manner to God.
