# Question 119: The Propagation of Man as to the Body

## Article 1: Whether Some Part of Food is Changed into True Human Nature

### Objection 1

Matthew 15:17 states "Whatsoever entereth into the mouth, goeth into the belly, and is cast out into the privy." What is expelled cannot have become genuinely human.

### Objection 2

Aristotle distinguishes flesh belonging to "species" from flesh belonging to "matter," noting the latter "comes and goes." Food-derived flesh is material and temporary, not essential to human nature.

### Objection 3

The "radical humor" constitutes true human nature and cannot be recovered if lost. Food could not restore it if genuinely transformed.

### Objection 4

If food became true human nature, losses could be perpetually replaced, making death avoidable—which is absurd.

### Objection 5

If food truly becomes human nature, all original bodily matter could eventually be replaced, destroying numerical identity throughout life.

### On the contrary

Augustine states: "The bodily food when corrupted...is changed into the texture of the members," which belongs to true human nature.

### I answer that

A thing's relationship to truth parallels its relationship to being. Nature may be considered generally (according to species) or individually. Some held the human form could exist only in one original matter from Adam, with food merely preventing radical humor consumption. This fails because: if a form can cease existing in its matter, it can begin in different matter; if all matter existed in one individual, only one human would exist; human growth requires transformation, not rarefaction alone.

Others proposed food matter becomes human nature secondarily only. This also fails because living bodies possess nutritive power generating their like in individual form—impossible if food weren't genuinely transformed. The soul imprints true human form through nutritive power upon assimilated food as effectively as generative power does. Food is necessary for both growth and renewal.

The correct teaching: food genuinely converts into true human nature by assuming the specific form of flesh, bones, and similar parts.

### Reply to Objection 1

Not "all" entering the mouth is expelled. Whatever forms from food dissolves through natural heat and exits through body pores.

### Reply to Objection 2

The distinction concerns the same flesh viewed differently. Considered according to species, flesh remains constant. Considered materially, flesh gradually depletes and renews—like fire in a furnace, where matter burns away and is replaced while form persists.

### Reply to Objection 3

The "radical humor" comprises what the species' virtue foundation rests upon; if removed, it cannot recover. The "nutritive humor" tends toward specific nature but hasn't perfectly received it. When such is lost, the species' virtue remains.

### Reply to Objection 4

Every transforming virtue in passible bodies weakens through continuous action. Initially sufficient for renewal and growth, then only renewal, then not even that—causing death. Like wine's virtue weakened by added water.

### Reply to Objection 5

As wood continuously burns with added wood, fire persists identically because addition passes into pre-existence. Living bodies similarly renew through nutrition while maintaining identity.

## Article 2: Whether Semen is Produced from Surplus Food

### Objection 1

Damascene states generation produces "from the substance of the begetter, that which is begotten." Since semen generates offspring, it derives from the begetter's substance.

### Objection 2

If semen derives from surplus food, sons would not resemble grandfathers or ancestors whose specific foods weren't in them.

### Objection 3

If semen came from surplus food—sometimes cow or pig flesh—men would relate more closely to these animals than to fathers.

### Objection 4

Augustine states we were in Adam "not only by seminal virtue, but also in the very substance of the body."

### On the contrary

Aristotle proves extensively that "semen is surplus food."

### I answer that

If human nature possesses virtue communicating form to alien matter, then food becomes similar through form communication. Natural order requires things reduce from potentiality to actuality gradually. Food first receives common virtue regarding all body parts, subsequently determining to particular parts.

Semen cannot be a solution from already-transformed member substance—such would either lose member nature (making it powerless) or retain it (limiting power to that part only). Therefore semen is the whole potentially, possessing power from the begetter's soul to produce the whole body. What nutritive power transforms becomes semen through generative power.

### Reply to Objection 1

Generation derives from the begetter's substance inasmuch as semen's virtue derives from the begetter's form and is in potentiality toward substance.

### Reply to Objection 2

Resemblance stems from the generating agent's form, not matter. The semen possesses virtue derived from the grandfather's soul through the father.

### Reply to Objection 3

Kinship relates not to matter but to forms' derivation.

### Reply to Objection 4

Augustine means both seminal virtue and corporeal substance existed in Adam as principle. Christ was in Adam according to "corporeal substance" (His Body's matter supplied by the Virgin Mother derived from Adam) but not seminal virtue, since His Body formed through the Holy Ghost's operation, not man's seminal virtue.
