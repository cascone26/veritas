# Question 111: The Action of the Angels on Man

## Article 1: Whether an Angel Can Enlighten Man

### Objection 1

Man is enlightened by faith, which Dionysius attributes to baptism. Since faith comes immediately from God according to Ephesians 2:8, man cannot be enlightened by angels.

### Objection 2

Both natural reason and God's revelation come immediately from God, as noted in the gloss on Romans 1:19. Therefore God enlightens man directly, not through angels.

### Objection 3

Whoever is enlightened must be conscious of being enlightened. Since man is not aware of angelic enlightenment, angels do not enlighten him.

### On the contrary

Dionysius states that divine revelation reaches men through the ministry of angels, and such revelation constitutes enlightenment.

### I answer that

Divine Providence orders lower things to be subject to higher things. Just as inferior angels are enlightened by superior angels, men are enlightened by them.

Enlightenment functions similarly yet differently between angels and humans. Among angels, the superior divides universal concepts according to the inferior's capacity. The human intellect, however, understands through turning to phantasms. Therefore, angels propose intelligible truth to humans through sensible similitudes, as Dionysius notes: "The divine ray cannot shine on us except shrouded by sacred veils."

### Reply to Objection 1

Faith requires two dispositions. First, the intellect's habit enabling it to obey the will tending toward divine truth—this comes from God alone. Second, faith requires that what is believed be proposed to the believer, accomplished principally through angels revealing divine things.

### Reply to Objection 2

Natural reason from God can be strengthened by an angel. The more the human intellect is strengthened, the higher intelligible truths can be derived from species received from creatures.

### Reply to Objection 3

One enlightened by an angel need not recognize the angel as the source, just as one need not know the intellect itself is the principle of understanding.

## Article 2: Whether Angels Can Change the Will of Man

### Objection 1

The gloss on Hebrews 1:7 notes that angels "burn away our vices," which could occur only if they changed the will.

### Objection 2

Bede states the devil "kindles" wicked thoughts, while Damascene says demons "put" wicked passions into men. Good angels similarly introduce good thoughts.

### Objection 3

Angels enlighten the human intellect through phantasms. Since the sensitive appetite serving will can similarly be changed using corporeal organs, angels should change the will as they enlighten the mind.

### On the contrary

Proverbs 21:1 states: "The heart of the king is in the hand of the Lord, whithersoever He will He shall turn it," indicating only God changes the will.

### I answer that

The will is moved in two ways. First, from within: since will's movement is the inclination toward the desired good, only God can move the will this way, as He alone grants intellectual nature its inclining power.

Second, the will is moved from without. Regarding angels, this occurs only through the good apprehended by intellect. Only God moves will efficaciously; angels and humans move will through persuasion.

Additionally, the human will is moved externally through passion in the sensitive appetite. Angels, able to rouse passions like concupiscence and anger, can move the will this way—though not by necessity, since will remains free to consent or resist passion.

### Reply to Objection 1

Angels burn away vices and incite to virtue through persuasion.

### Reply to Objection 2

Demons cannot put thoughts in minds by causing them internally. They are called thought-kindlers because they incite thinking through desire, via persuasion or passion-arousal.

### Reply to Objection 3

The human will can will something following reason's judgment rather than sensitive appetite's passion. The comparison therefore fails.

## Article 3: Whether an Angel Can Change Man's Imagination

### Objection 1

Fantasy is "a motion caused by sense in act." If an angel caused this motion, it would not be caused by actual sense, contradicting fantasy's nature.

### Objection 2

Forms in imagination are spiritual and nobler than forms in sensible matter. Since angels cannot impress forms on sensible matter, they cannot impress forms on imagination.

### Objection 3

Augustine states one spirit can communicate knowledge through images by intermingling. But angels cannot mingle with human imagination.

### Objection 4

In imaginative vision, man cleaves to similitudes as to things themselves, involving deception. Good angels cannot cause deception.

### On the contrary

Angels reveal things in dreams, as shown when an angel appeared to Joseph (Matthew 1:20, 2:13-19).

### I answer that

Both good and bad angels can move human imagination by their natural power. Corporeal nature obeys angels regarding local movement. Imaginative apparitions result from animal spirits' and humors' local movement. Aristotle explains that during sleep, blood descends to sensation's principle, carrying impressions that produce appearance as if external objects were affecting it. Since natural disturbance, human will, good angels, and bad angels can all produce this effect, angels can do so with or without bodily sense alienation.

### Reply to Objection 1

Imagination originates from actual sense but can operate from internally preserved impressions.

### Reply to Objection 2

Angels change imagination not by impressing forms never sensed but through local movement of spirits and humors.

### Reply to Objection 3

The angel shows man what he knows through effects produced in imagination, but not the manner of his knowing.

### Reply to Objection 4

An angel causing imaginative vision sometimes simultaneously enlightens intellect, enabling recognition of what images signify—preventing deception. Deception results from intellect's defect, not from the angel.

## Article 4: Whether an Angel Can Change the Human Senses

### Objection 1

Sensitive operation is vital, not proceeding from external principle. Therefore angels cannot cause sensitive operation.

### Objection 2

Sensitive operation is nobler than nutritive. Since angels cannot change natural forms, they cannot change sensitive power.

### Objection 3

Senses are naturally moved by sensible objects. Angels cannot change nature's order.

### On the contrary

Angels struck Sodomites with blindness (Genesis 19:11) and blinded the Syrians Eliseus led to Samaria (2 Kings 6:18).

### I answer that

Senses are changed two ways: externally, when affected by sensible objects; internally, when spirits and humors are disturbed. Angels can change senses both ways naturally. An angel can present sensible objects formed by nature or assuming a body. Angels can also move spirits and humors internally, thereby changing senses variously.

### Reply to Objection 1

The sensitive operation's interior principle cannot exist without internal principles, yet this interior principle is moved many ways by external principles.

### Reply to Objection 2

Through internal spirit and humor movement, angels can effect changes in the nutritive, appetitive, sensitive, and other corporeal-organ-using powers' acts.

### Reply to Objection 3

Angels can work outside particular nature-orders since they are not subject to those orders, though they cannot work outside the entire creature-order.
