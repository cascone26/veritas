# Question 109: The Ordering of the Bad Angels

## Article 1: Whether There Are Orders Among the Demons

### Objection 1

Order belongs to good, while disorder belongs to evil. Since the bad angels possess nothing orderly, there cannot be orders among them.

### Objection 2

Angelic orders exist within a hierarchy defined as "a holy principality." Demons lack holiness entirely, so they cannot belong to any hierarchy.

### Objection 3

If demons fell from every angelic order, they should be called by those order names. Yet we never call them "Seraphim" or "Thrones," so they belong to no order.

### On the contrary

Scripture states: "Our wrestling is against principalities and powers, against the rulers of the world of this darkness" (Ephesians 6:12).

### I answer that

Order in angels exists according to natural grade and grace. Grace has two states: imperfect (merit) and perfect (glory). Considering the perfection of glory, demons never belonged to angelic orders. Regarding imperfect grace, demons were once in the orders but fell away. Considering natural order, demons remain in those orders since they retain their natural gifts, as Dionysius teaches.

### Reply to Objection 1

Evil cannot exist without good. Thus demons possess order as creatures possessing good nature.

### Reply to Objection 2

God's ordering of demons is sacred from His perspective, though demons' will makes their order profane since they abuse their nature for evil.

### Reply to Objection 3

Names like "Seraphim" derive from charity, "Thrones" from Divine indwelling, and "Dominations" from liberty—all opposed to sin. Therefore these names do not apply to sinful angels.

## Article 2: Whether Among the Demons There Is Precedence

### Objection 1

Precedence follows justice. Since demons wholly lack justice, no precedence exists among them.

### Objection 2

Precedence requires obedience and subjection, which require concord. Scripture says: "Among the proud there are always contentions" (Proverbs 13:10), so demons lack precedence.

### Objection 3

Precedence derives either from nature, sin, or punishment. It cannot follow nature since subjection comes from subsequent sin. Therefore no precedence exists.

### On the contrary

A gloss on 1 Corinthians 15:24 states: "While the world lasts, angels preside over angels, men over men, and demons over demons."

### I answer that

Since action follows nature, where natures are subordinate, their actions must be subordinate. Demons are naturally ordered beneath others; thus their actions are subject to those above them. God's wisdom "ordereth all things sweetly" (Wisdom 8:1), leaving nothing disordered.

### Reply to Objection 1

Demonic authority rests not on their justice but on God's justice ordering all things.

### Reply to Objection 2

Demonic concord arises from shared wickedness and mutual hatred of humans and God's justice, not from friendship.

### Reply to Objection 3

Demons are naturally unequal, establishing natural precedence among them—unlike humans who are naturally equal. The inferior's subjection to superiors increases the superiors' unhappiness, since ruling in evil constitutes greater misery.

## Article 3: Whether There Is Enlightenment in the Demons

### Objection 1

Superior demons excel in natural knowledge, so they can enlighten inferior demons.

### Objection 2

A body abundant in light can enlighten a deficient body. Superior demons possess greater natural light, so they can enlighten inferior demons.

### On the contrary

Enlightenment involves cleansing and perfecting. Scripture asks: "What can be made clean by the unclean?" (Sirach 34:4). Therefore demons cannot enlighten.

### I answer that

Proper enlightenment cannot occur among demons. Enlightenment properly involves manifesting truth regarding God, Who enlightens every intellect. Demonic perversity does not order others toward God but away from Divine order. While one demon may communicate concepts to another through speech, this is not enlightenment proper.

### Reply to Objection 1

Not all manifestation of truth constitutes enlightenment—only that which directs toward God.

### Reply to Objection 2

Natural knowledge requires no necessary manifestation of truth, since all within their natural knowledge is known from the beginning. Thus superior demons' greater natural light does not prove they enlighten others.

## Article 4: Whether the Good Angels Have Precedence Over the Bad Angels

### Objection 1

Bad angels, being darkness, are not enlightened by good angels, so good angels do not rule over bad ones.

### Objection 2

Superiors bear responsibility for subjects' negligence. Since demons perform much evil, charging good angels as superiors with negligence is inadmissible.

### Objection 3

If demons fell from every order, many demons exceed many good angels naturally. Thus good angels cannot have precedence over all bad angels.

### On the contrary

Augustine states: "The treacherous and sinful spirit of life is ruled by the rational, pious, and just spirit of life." Gregory teaches that Powers are "the angels to whose charge are subjected the hostile powers."

### I answer that

Precedence originates in God and is shared by creatures according to their closeness to Him. The greatest perfection—enjoying God—belongs to holy angels; demons lack this. Therefore good angels have precedence over bad angels and rule them.

### Reply to Objection 1

Holy angels make Divine mysteries known to bad angels when Divine justice requires them to act for punishing evildoers or testing the good.

### Reply to Objection 2

As God permits some evil by bad agents for subsequent good, good angels do not entirely restrain demons from causing harm.

### Reply to Objection 3

An angel naturally inferior to demons may preside over them because Divine justice's power, to which good angels cleave, exceeds natural angelic power. Similarly, "the spiritual man judgeth all things" (1 Corinthians 2:15).
