# Question 98: The Preservation of the Species

## Article 1: Whether Generation Existed in the State of Innocence

### Objection 1
Generation and corruption are contraries. Since corruption wouldn't exist, neither would generation.

### Objection 2
Humans would have lived eternally, making generation unnecessary.

### Objection 3
Generation multiplies humanity, requiring property division contradicting natural common ownership.

### On the contrary
"Increase and multiply, and fill the earth" (Genesis 1:28).

### I answer that
Generation would have occurred for multiplying the human race. Humans occupy a middle position: incorruptible souls and corruptible bodies. Nature preserves species through generation in corruptible things. For humans, even individuals constitute nature's purpose through incorruptible souls.

### Reply to Objection 1
Though corruptible in nature, the body could be preserved by the soul. Generation remained proper.

### Reply to Objection 2
Generation served individual multiplication rather than species preservation.

### Reply to Objection 3
Ordered wills would use shared possessions harmoniously.

## Article 2: Whether Generation Would Have Occurred Through Coition

### Objection 1
The first human resembled an angel. In resurrection humans neither marry nor are given in marriage.

### Objection 2
First parents would have coupled in paradise if coition existed, contradicting Scripture.

### Objection 3
Intercourse makes humans beast-like through excessive pleasure.

### Objection 4
Virginal integrity is corrupted through intercourse.

### On the contrary
God created male and female before sin. Sexual distinction serves generation. Woman was created as helper, fitting only for generation.

### I answer that
What is natural to humans wasn't acquired or forfeited by sin. Coition is natural to humans through animal life. Two elements characterize it: natural male-female union (necessary for generation) and excessive concupiscence (absent in innocence when lower powers obeyed reason). Augustine states: "Offspring could be begotten without concupiscence, with calm."

### Reply to Objection 1
Paradise humans resembled angels mentally while maintaining animal bodies. Resurrection differs fundamentally.

### Reply to Objection 2
Parents didn't couple because expelled shortly after woman's creation, or awaited special timing.

### Reply to Objection 3
In innocence, reason would regulate everything. Sensual pleasure would intensify through greater purity, but concupiscence wouldn't cling immoderately.

### Reply to Objection 4
Augustine explains intercourse would preserve virginal integrity. Conception would result from deliberate action rather than lustful desire.
