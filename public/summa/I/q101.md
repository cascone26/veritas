# Question 101: The Condition of the Offspring as Regards Knowledge

## Article 1: Whether Children in the State of Innocence Would Have Been Born with Perfect Knowledge

### Objection 1

Children would have been born with perfect knowledge, since Adam would have begotten children like himself. Adam possessed perfect knowledge (I:94:3), so his offspring would have inherited this perfection.

### Objection 2

Ignorance results from sin, as Bede teaches (I-II:85:3). Since ignorance is the privation of knowledge, children before sin would have possessed perfect knowledge from birth.

### Objection 3

Children would have been gifted with righteousness from birth. Knowledge directs our actions and is necessary for righteousness, so they would have possessed knowledge as well.

### On the contrary

The human soul is naturally "like a blank tablet on which nothing is written," according to Aristotle (De Anima iii, 4). Since the soul's nature remains constant, children's souls would have been without knowledge at birth.

### I answer that

Following the principle that we rely on authority for supernatural matters but on nature's ordinary course otherwise, knowledge is naturally acquired through the senses. The soul is united to the body to utilize the sensitive powers for knowledge acquisition. Therefore, children in innocence would not have been born with perfect knowledge, but would have acquired it gradually through discovery and learning without difficulty.

### Reply to Objection 1

Perfect knowledge was an individual accident of Adam as the father and instructor of humanity. He would have begotten children resembling him only in natural or universally conferred accidents, not in this exceptional perfection.

### Reply to Objection 2

Ignorance is privation of knowledge that ought to exist at a particular time. Children would have possessed knowledge appropriate to their state, so no ignorance—only nescience regarding certain matters—would have affected them, as existed even in holy angels per Dionysius (Coel. Hier. vii).

### Reply to Objection 3

Children would have possessed sufficient knowledge from universal principles of righteousness to direct their virtuous actions. This knowledge would have been far more complete than natural knowledge now, as with other universal principles.

## Article 2: Whether Children Would Have Had Perfect Use of Reason at Birth

### Objection 1

Children lack perfect use of reason now because the soul is weighed down by the body. Scripture states the "corruptible body is a load upon the soul" (Wisdom 9:15). Without post-sin corruption, children would have possessed perfect use of reason at birth.

### Objection 2

Animals use their natural powers at birth—lambs immediately flee wolves. Humans in innocence would certainly have possessed perfect reason from birth.

### On the contrary

Nature proceeds from imperfect to perfect in all generated things. Therefore, children would not have possessed perfect use of reason from birth.

### I answer that

The use of reason depends upon the sensitive powers (I:84:7). When senses are impaired—as in sleep or delirium—reason's use diminishes. Children's brains contain excessive humidity, hindering sensitive power function and consequently reason's use. Therefore, in innocence, children would not have possessed perfect use of reason at birth, though they would have exercised it more perfectly than now regarding their particular state, as explained regarding limb use (I:99:1).

### Reply to Objection 1

The corruptible body burdens the soul by hindering reason's use across all human life stages.

### Reply to Objection 2

Even other animals lack perfect use of natural powers at birth—birds must teach their young to fly. Moreover, humans suffer special impediment from brain humidity, as noted above (I:99:1).
