# Question 115: The Action of the Corporeal Creature

## Article 1: Whether a Body Can Be Active

### Objection 1

Augustine states: "There are things that are acted upon, but do not act; such are bodies." Therefore bodies possess no active power.

### Objection 2

Every agent except God requires a subject susceptible to its action. Since no substance exists below corporeal substance, bodies cannot be agents.

### Objection 3

Corporeal substance is limited by quantity, which hinders movement and action. Heavier bodies are harder to move, proving bodies lack active power.

### Objection 4

Bodies are most composite and therefore most remote from God, who is supremely simple. Thus bodies cannot be active.

### Objection 5

If bodies act, their action produces either substantial or accidental forms. Neither seems possible since accidents cannot cause substances, and accidents do not transcend their subjects.

### On the contrary

Dionysius affirms that corporeal fire demonstrates "its greatness in its action and power" (Coel. Hier. xv).

### I answer that

Sensory experience demonstrates some bodies are active. Avicebron denied all bodily action, arguing spiritual powers produce all effects. Yet this merely shows bodies are not universal agents, not that they lack agency entirely. A body acts insofar as it exists in actuality upon a body insofar as it exists in potentiality.

### Reply to Objection 1

Augustine's statement concerns corporeal nature as a whole, which has no inferior nature upon which to act. Yet one body is inferior to another through existing in potentiality to what another possesses in actuality.

### Reply to Objection 2

Primary matter as pure potentiality is purely passive. Bodies comprise both potentiality and actuality, making them both active and passive.

### Reply to Objection 3

Quantity does not wholly prevent corporeal form from acting. Action proceeds from potentiality to actuality, not from local movement.

### Reply to Objection 4

Bodies are not most distant from God. Primary matter—pure potentiality—is most distant and wholly inactive.

### Reply to Objection 5

Active qualities like heat, though accidents, act through the substantial form as their instrument. Natural heat as the soul's instrument generates flesh (substantial form). No contradiction exists in accidents exceeding their subjects in action, only in being.

## Article 2: Whether Seminal Virtues Exist in Corporeal Matter

### Objection 1

"Virtue" implies something spiritual. In corporeal matter, nothing exists spiritually. Therefore seminal virtues cannot exist there.

### Objection 2

Augustine states demons employ hidden seeds through local movement. Bodies, not virtues, can be locally moved.

### Objection 3

Seeds are active principles. Since matter is not active, seminal virtues cannot exist in corporeal matter.

### Objection 4

"Causal virtues" seemingly suffice for production. Seminal virtues seem unnecessary.

### On the contrary

Augustine declares: "Of all things generated in visible fashion, certain seeds lie hidden in corporeal things" (De Trin. iii, 8).

### I answer that

Augustine fittingly termed all active and passive principles of natural generation "seminal virtues." These exist in ordered levels: principally in God's Word as typal ideas; in world elements as universal causes; in particular effects as particular causes; and in seeds produced from animals and plants.

### Reply to Objection 1

These principles merit the title "virtues" respecting their origin as effects of typal ideas.

### Reply to Objection 2

These virtues exist in certain corporeal parts. When demons employ them with local movement, they are said to employ seeds.

### Reply to Objection 3

Male seed is the active principle; female seed the passive. Both merit the "seed" designation.

### Reply to Objection 4

Typal ideas merit "causal virtues" designation but not strictly "seminal virtues." Miracles exceed natural active virtues but work within God's ordained passive potentialities.

## Article 3: Whether Heavenly Bodies Cause Inferior Bodies' Production

### Objection 1

Damascene states heavenly bodies "are not the cause of generation or corruption: they are rather signs."

### Objection 2

Inferior bodies possess passive matter and active agents (heat, cold). Heavenly bodies prove unnecessary.

### Objection 3

Inferior bodies generate through qualities absent from heavenly bodies. Therefore heavenly bodies cannot cause inferior effects.

### Objection 4

Heavenly bodies don't determine sex; twins under identical constellations show differing sexes.

### On the contrary

Augustine affirms: "Bodies of grosser nature are ruled in order by those of subtler power." Dionysius teaches the sun's light "conduces to generation."

### I answer that

Throughout nature, all movement proceeds from the immovable. Heavenly bodies, being most immovable (moving only locally), must be causes of various movements in inferior bodies. Inferior active principles comprise merely elemental qualities (hot, cold). Since substantial forms require more than material dispositions, a movable principle (heavenly bodies) becomes necessary. Aristotle states: "Man and the sun generate man."

### Reply to Objection 1

Damascene opposes those claiming heavenly bodies are gods.

### Reply to Objection 2

Inferior agents act as heavenly bodies' instruments, disposing matter for forms exceeding elemental qualities' capacity.

### Reply to Objection 3

Heavenly bodies' likeness to inferior bodies consists in universal power—all inferior generation is contained within them.

### Reply to Objection 4

Heavenly effects vary with matter's disposition, explaining sex differences in twins.

## Article 4: Whether Heavenly Bodies Cause Human Actions

### Objection 1

Spiritual substances moving heavenly bodies surpass human souls, seemingly impressing effects causing human actions.

### Objection 2

Human actions' multiformity reduces to heavenly movements' uniformity.

### Objection 3

Astrologers frequently predict human actions correctly, implying heavenly causation.

### On the contrary

Damascene declares: "Heavenly bodies are by no means the cause of human actions."

### I answer that

Heavenly bodies directly act upon corporeal bodies and accidentally upon soul powers using corporeal organs. If intellect and will were corporeal organ powers, heavenly bodies would necessarily cause human actions—but this is manifestly false and contrary to free will.

Indirectly, heavenly bodies reach intellect and will through inferior powers. The will does not necessarily follow inferior appetite inclination; it retains power to follow or repress passions. Thus heavenly impressions less influence the will than the intellect.

### Reply to Objection 1

Spiritual substances act on the intellect through enlightenment and cannot compel the will.

### Reply to Objection 2

Action multiformity from intellect and will reduces to Divine intellect and will, not heavenly movements.

### Reply to Objection 3

Most men follow sensitive appetite passions, in which heavenly bodies cooperate. Thus astrologers often predict truth generally. Nothing prevents man resisting passions through free will. "The wise man is stronger than the stars."

## Article 5: Whether Heavenly Bodies Can Act Upon Demons

### Objection 1

Demons harass men according to lunar phases. Therefore demons are subject to heavenly bodies.

### Objection 2

Necromancers observe constellations to invoke demons.

### Objection 3

Demons are confined to inferior bodies (herbs, stones, animals). Therefore they are subject to heavenly bodies.

### On the contrary

Demons surpass heavenly bodies in nature's order. The agent surpasses the patient.

### I answer that

Demons are spiritual substances unattached to bodies. Thus they are subject to heavenly body action neither essentially nor accidentally.

### Reply to Objection 1

Demons harass according to lunar phases to defame God's creature or because the brain, being most moist, is most subject to the moon's influence—demons employ natural dispositions.

### Reply to Objection 2

Demons summoned through constellations come to induce belief in star divinity or because matter is better disposed under certain constellations.

### Reply to Objection 3

Demons are enticed through objects not as animals by food but as spirits by signs—honor tokens owed to God, whom they covet.

## Article 6: Whether Heavenly Bodies Impose Necessity Upon Subject Things

### Objection 1

Heavenly bodies constitute sufficient causes. Being necessary beings, their effects necessarily follow.

### Objection 2

Heavenly bodies' power entirely subdues inferior matter. Thus effects necessarily result.

### Objection 3

Any hindering cause reduces to heavenly principles (themselves necessary). Thus all effects result necessarily.

### On the contrary

Aristotle states many observed signs should not be fulfilled.

### I answer that

The will's action can hinder heavenly effects. Regarding natural things: some causes produce effects in most cases but fail in minorities. A hindering cause clashing with an ordered cause clashes accidentally, and this accidental clashing has no cause. Example: a burning body falling from the sky and alighting on combustible matter is accidental, not caused by heavenly bodies. Thus not all heavenly effects result necessarily.

### Reply to Objection 1

Heavenly bodies cause effects through particular inferior causes, which can fail.

### Reply to Objection 2

Heavenly body power requires determinate matter disposition for effect production. Distance and other conditions can hinder effects.

### Reply to Objection 3

Two causes' accidental clashing is not reducible to heavenly body causality.
