# Question 92: The Production of the Woman

## Article 1: Whether the Woman Should Have Been Made in the First Production of Things

### Objection 1
The Philosopher states the female is a misbegotten male. Nothing defective should exist in creation's first production.

### Objection 2
Subjection resulted from sin. Since woman naturally possesses less strength and dignity, she should not have been created before sin.

### Objection 3
God foreknew woman would become an occasion of sin. He should have prevented her creation.

### On the contrary
"It is not good for man to be alone; let us make him a helper like to himself" (Genesis 2:18).

### I answer that
Woman's creation was necessary as a "helper" in generation specifically. In perfect animals, males possess active power while females possess passive power. Separating male and female while maintaining unity for procreation suited humans who pursue nobler intellectual operations.

### Reply to Objection 1
Regarding individual nature, woman is defective—male seed naturally tends toward masculine reproduction. However, concerning human nature generally, woman is intended by nature for generation.

### Reply to Objection 2
Servile subjection arose after sin. Economic/civil subjection (governance for subjects' welfare) existed before sin. Innocence didn't eliminate inequality.

### Reply to Objection 3
Had God removed all sin-occasions, creation would remain imperfect. God's power directs all evil toward good ends.

## Article 2: Whether the Woman Should Have Been Made from Man

### Objection 1
Female animals weren't made from males. Therefore, woman shouldn't have been either.

### Objection 2
Man was made from earth's slime. Woman should have been similarly created.

### Objection 3
Close kinship disqualifies for matrimony. Woman shouldn't have been made from man.

### On the contrary
"He created of him...a helpmate like to himself, that is, woman" (Sirach 17:5).

### I answer that
Woman's creation from man was fitting: it granted man singular dignity as humanity's origin; man would love woman more intensely knowing she was from himself; it signified her as his principle in domestic life; sacramentally it prefigured the Church's origin from Christ.

### Reply to Objection 1
Clarified above.

### Reply to Objection 2
Divine power generates identical species from any matter.

### Reply to Objection 3
Eve wasn't produced through natural generation. She isn't called Adam's daughter.

## Article 3: Whether the Woman Was Fittingly Made from Man's Rib

### Objection 1
A rib was much smaller than woman's body. Enlarging it requires addition or rarefaction.

### Objection 2
Adam's rib belonged to his bodily integrity. Removing it left him imperfect.

### Objection 3
Rib removal causes pain. No pain existed before sin.

### On the contrary
"God built the rib, which He took from Adam, into a woman" (Genesis 2:22).

### I answer that
Woman's formation from man's rib was proper: it signifies social union—woman shouldn't dominate man (not from his head) nor serve as slave (not from his feet). Sacramentally it prefigures Christ: from His side flowed blood and water establishing the Church.

### Reply to Objection 1
Addition must have occurred through creation or conversion, like Christ feeding thousands.

### Reply to Objection 2
Adam's rib belonged to his perfection as humanity's principle, not merely as individual—like semen belongs to the begetter.

## Article 4: Whether the Woman Was Formed Immediately by God

### Objection 1
No individual is produced immediately by God from another of the same species.

### Objection 2
Augustine teaches corporeal things are governed through angels.

### Objection 3
Woman's body pre-existed in causal virtues.

### On the contrary
Augustine states: "God alone could form or build up the woman from the man's rib."

### I answer that
Natural generation requires determinate matter. From other matter, no human individual can naturally generate. Only God produces effects outside nature's ordinary course. Therefore, God alone could create woman from man's rib.

### Reply to Objection 1
This applies to natural generation only.

### Reply to Objection 2
Angels certainly didn't form her from man's rib, just as they didn't form man from earth.

### Reply to Objection 3
Woman pre-existed in passive potentiality only—relative to God's active potentiality.
