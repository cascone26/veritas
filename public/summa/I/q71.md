# Question 71: The Work of the Fifth Day

## Article 1: Whether the work of the fifth day is fittingly described?

### Objection 1
Water's power does not suffice to produce all kinds of fishes and birds, since many of them are generated from seed. Therefore, the scriptural description of waters bringing forth creatures seems unfitting.

### Objection 2
Fishes and birds contain more earth than water in their composition, as their bodies tend naturally to the earth and rest upon it. Thus they should not be described as produced from water.

### Objection 3
Since fishes move in water and birds in air, if fishes are produced from water, then birds should be produced from air, not water.

### Objection 4
Not all fishes creep; some, like seals, have feet and walk on land. The description of fishes as creeping creatures is therefore incomplete.

### Objection 5
Land animals are more perfect than birds and fishes, having more distinct limbs, and generation of a higher order through producing living young rather than eggs. The more perfect should precede in nature's order.

### On the contrary
The authority of Scripture suffices.

### I answer that
The order of adorning creation mirrors the order of distinguishing it. Since the second day addressed water as an intermediate body, the fifth day—the middle of three adornment days—appropriately adorns water through producing birds and fishes. This correspondence parallels how the fourth day mentions lights to echo the first day's creation of light.

Augustine differs from other writers, holding that waters produced creatures potentially rather than actually on the fifth day.

### Reply to Objection 1
While Avicenna claimed animals generate from elemental mixtures without seed, this contradicts nature's determinate operation. Initially, God's Word acted as the active principle, producing creatures from elements either actually or virtually through powers bestowed upon matter.

### Reply to Objection 2
Considering birds and fishes intrinsically, earth must predominate for proper bodily balance. However, as by nature constituted to move with certain specific motions, they possess affinity with their respective elements, explaining the generation description.

### Reply to Objection 3
Air, being imperceptible to senses, is not enumerated separately but grouped with water (lower air contains watery exhalations) and heaven (upper air). Since birds fly in the lower part of the air, their production associates with water.

### Reply to Objection 4
Nature progresses through intermediate forms between extremes. Intermediate creatures between aquatic and aerial animals are reckoned as belonging to that class to which they are most allied. The mention of great whales encompasses such intermediate forms.

### Reply to Objection 5
The production order references the order of those bodies which they are set to adorn, rather than to the superiority of the animals themselves. Generation itself proceeds through imperfection toward perfection.
