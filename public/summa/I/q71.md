# Question 71: The Work of the Fifth Day

## Article 1: Whether the work of the fifth day is fittingly described?

### Objection 1
The waters cannot produce all types of fish and birds since many are "generated from seed." Therefore, the scriptural description stating that waters should bring forth creatures with life seems inaccurate.

### Objection 2
Fish and birds contain more earth than water in their composition, as evidenced by their bodies naturally tending downward. Thus attributing their creation to water alone seems unfitting.

### Objection 3
Fish move through water while birds move through air. If fish originate from water, birds should originate "from the air, and not from the waters."

### Objection 4
Not all fish creep; some creatures like seals possess feet and walk on land, making the description incomplete.

### Objection 5
Land animals surpass birds and fish in perfection through more distinct limbs and superior reproduction methods. The more perfect should precede in the natural order.

### On the Contrary
"Suffices the authority of Scripture."

### I Answer That
The adornment work follows the order of distinction work. Since the second day addresses water as an intermediate body, the fifth day similarly adorns this intermediate realm through birds and fish. The fifth day thus corresponds to the second day, with both mentioning water and firmament.

Augustine differs from other writers, holding that waters produced these creatures "potentially" rather than actually on the fifth day.

### Reply to Objection 1
God's Word originally produced animals from elemental matter, either in actuality or virtually. The power resides not inherently in water or earth themselves, but in the capacity originally granted to elements for generation.

### Reply to Objection 2
While earthly elements predominate in composition, creatures possess "special affinity with the bodies in which they move," justifying their attribution to water.

### Reply to Objection 3
Air remains imperceptible and is enumerated with water and heaven. Birds inhabit the lower air region, hence described as flying "beneath the firmament," making their attribution to water appropriate.

### Reply to Objection 4
Nature transitions between extremes through intermediate forms. Creatures intermediate between air and water animals are classified with whichever class they most resemble. The phrase about "great whales" encompasses such intermediate types.

### Reply to Objection 5
The production order reflects the bodies being adorned rather than animal superiority. Additionally, generation itself proceeds from less perfect toward more perfect forms.
