# Question 110: How Angels Act on Bodies

## Article 1: Whether the Corporeal Creature is Governed by Angels

### Objection 1

Bodies possess determinate modes of action from their divinely-bestowed nature, so they require no governance from angels.

### Objection 2

Some corporeal things are superior and others inferior to each other, so they need not be governed by angels.

### Objection 3

If corporeal creatures were ruled by angels, there would be as many angelic offices as species of things, creating more than nine angelic orders.

### On the contrary

Augustine states: "All bodies are ruled by the rational spirit of life," and Gregory affirms that "in this visible world nothing takes place without the agency of the invisible creature" (Dial. iv, 6).

### I answer that

In both human and natural affairs, particular powers are governed by universal powers. Angels with more universal knowledge rule inferior angels. Since corporeal power is more particular than spiritual substance—corporeal forms being individualized by matter—all bodies are ruled by angels. This doctrine is affirmed by Church teachers and philosophers acknowledging incorporeal substances.

### Reply to Objection 1

Bodies act only when moved, since it belongs to bodies not to act unless set in motion. Therefore corporeal creatures must be moved by spiritual creatures.

### Reply to Objection 2

Many effects occur in inferior bodies beyond natural corporeal actions, requiring immediate angelic governance of lower bodies, not merely heavenly ones.

### Reply to Objection 3

The nine angelic orders remain unchanged since orders are distinguished by general offices. The order of Virtues encompasses angels presiding over purely corporeal creatures through whom miracles occur.

## Article 2: Whether Corporeal Matter Obeys the Mere Will of an Angel

### Objection 1

Angelic power exceeds the soul's power. Since matter obeys the soul's conception (human bodies change regarding heat, cold, health), matter should obey angelic conception more readily.

### Objection 2

Whatever an inferior power accomplishes, a superior power can accomplish. Bodies transform corporeal matter. Therefore angels can transform matter more effectively.

### Objection 3

All corporeal nature is under angelic administration, making bodies instrumental to angels. Instruments receive principal effects from principal agents. Thus substantial forms result from angelic power.

### On the contrary

Augustine warns: "It is not to be thought that this visible matter obeys these rebel angels; for it obeys God alone."

### I answer that

Aristotle proves that what is properly made is the composite—what subsists. Since what is made resembles the maker, and natural things are made either by composite agents or by God alone who possesses the whole composite in power, all matter-informing occurs either immediately from God or from corporeal agents, but not immediately from angels.

### Reply to Objection 1

The soul unites to body as form, so the body is naturally subject to the soul's formal influence. Angels lack this connection with natural bodies.

### Reply to Objection 2

Angels change matter more excellently by moving corporeal agents themselves as superior causes.

### Reply to Objection 3

Angelic power may produce natural effects exceeding corporeal agents' capacity without matter obeying an angel's mere will. A cook regulating fire produces dishes fire alone could not; this exceeds fire's power but remains natural action.

## Article 3: Whether Bodies Obey Angels Regarding Local Motion

### Objection 1

Local motion in natural bodies follows their forms. Angels do not cause natural bodies' forms. Therefore angels cannot cause local motion.

### Objection 2

Local motion is the first of all movements. Since angels cannot cause movements through formal matter-changes, they cannot cause local motion.

### Objection 3

Natural bodies lack vital principles, so they do not obey angels in local motion.

### On the contrary

Augustine states angels use corporeal seed to produce effects, which requires causing local movement.

### I answer that

As Dionysius teaches: "Divine wisdom has joined the ends of the first to the principles of the second." Among corporeal movements, local motion is most perfect, affecting only extrinsic location. Corporeal nature has natural aptitude for immediate spiritual motion regarding place. Philosophers held supreme bodies are moved locally by spiritual substances, as the soul moves body primarily through local motion.

### Reply to Objection 1

Local movements exist in bodies beyond those resulting from substantial forms—the sea's ebb and flow follows lunar influence, not water's form.

### Reply to Objection 2

By causing local motion as primary movement, angels thereby cause other movements, employing corporeal agents as workmen employ fire to soften iron.

### Reply to Objection 3

Angelic power is not limited as the soul's is. The soul's motive power restricts to unified, vivified bodies. Angelic power, unrestricted to any body, moves locally bodies unjoined to it.

## Article 4: Whether Angels Can Work Miracles

### Objection 1

Gregory states those spirits called Virtues usually perform signs and miracles.

### Objection 2

Augustine indicates magicians work miracles through demon-contracts, Christians through Divine justice.

### Objection 3

Augustine suggests "all things we see happen may be brought about by the lower powers that dwell in our atmosphere."

### Objection 4

Superior power is not subject to inferior cause-order. Angels work outside corporeal agents' order.

### On the contrary

Scripture states of God: "Who alone doth great wonders" (Psalm 135:4).

### I answer that

A true miracle occurs outside the order of all created nature—not merely outside particular nature's order. Whatever an angel accomplishes through its own power accords with created nature's order, thus remaining non-miraculous. God alone works miracles.

### Reply to Objection 1

Angels are said to work miracles because God works miracles at their request, or because they minister in miracles.

### Reply to Objection 2

Since we know not all created nature's power, effects outside known created nature by unknown power are called miracles relative to ourselves, not absolutely.

### Reply to Objection 3

Spiritual powers effect visible-world happenings by employing corporeal seeds through local movement.

### Reply to Objection 4

Although angels accomplish things outside corporeal nature's order, they cannot act outside the whole created order—essential to true miracles.
