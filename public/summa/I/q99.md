# Question 99: The Condition of the Offspring as to the Body

## Article 1: Whether Children Would Have Had Perfect Bodily Strength at Birth

### Objection 1
Bodily weakness reflects mental weakness. Innocence excludes both.

### Objection 2
Many animals possess functional limbs at birth. Infant weakness appears punitive.

### Objection 3
Inability causes suffering, contradicting the sinless state.

### Objection 4
Elderly weakness mirrors infant weakness. Without aging defects, infant defects should be absent.

### On the contrary
All generated beings begin imperfect.

### I answer that
Infants would lack immediate bodily capability. Humans possess larger brains; infant brain humidity prevents full nerve function. However, infants would possess strength sufficient for age-appropriate activities like nursing.

### Reply to Objection 1
Augustine references weakness regarding acts proper to infancy itself.

### Reply to Objection 2
Animals with immediate functionality have dry brains and limited operations.

### Reply to Objection 3
Innocent beings would desire only things appropriate to their condition.

### Reply to Objection 4
Infantile defects from generation would exist, but not senile defects causing deterioration.

## Article 2: Whether Women Would Have Been Born in the Primitive State

### Objection 1
Aristotle calls woman a "misbegotten male." Nothing unnatural would occur in innocence.

### Objection 2
Male procreative force, perfect without defect, would always produce males.

### Objection 3
The first couple's immortality eliminated the need for female births.

### On the contrary
God established both sexes in human nature.

### I answer that
Complete human nature encompasses sexual diversity. Different grades serve universal perfection. Therefore both sexes would reproduce.

### Reply to Objection 1
Woman represents departure from individual pattern, not universal nature's purpose.

### Reply to Objection 2
Female generation results from external causes. In innocence, parents could determine offspring sex through will.

### Reply to Objection 3
All should procreate, not merely first parents, suggesting equal proportions.
