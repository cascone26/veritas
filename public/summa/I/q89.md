# Question 89: The Knowledge of the Separated Soul

## Article 1: Whether the Separated Soul Can Understand

### Objection 1
The Philosopher states that understanding is corrupted with its interior principle. Death corrupts all human interior principles.

### Objection 2
The human soul cannot understand when senses or imagination are destroyed. Death destroys both.

### Objection 3
The separated soul cannot understand through innate species, abstracted species, retained species, or divinely infused species.

### On the contrary
The Philosopher affirms that if the soul had no proper operation, it could not be separated from the body. Since the soul is separated, it must have proper operations, chiefly intellectual understanding.

### I answer that
The soul's nature does not change at death—only its mode of existence. When united to the body, the soul understands through phantasms. When separated, it has a different mode suited to separate substances.

God ordered human souls to require bodies because human intellects are the lowest among intellectual substances. By requiring embodied learning from sensible particulars, human souls gain proper knowledge. When separated, the soul understands through participated species from Divine light, which all separate substances share.

### Reply to Objection 1
The Philosopher spoke of understanding as bodily motion, before distinguishing intellect from sense.

### Reply to Objection 3
The separated soul understands through participated species from Divine illumination. This is natural knowledge, not grace.

## Article 2: Whether the Separated Soul Understands Separate Substances

### Objection 1
The embodied soul does not understand separate substances. The separated soul, being less perfect, cannot either.

### Objection 2
Separate substances cannot be known by presence (only God enters the soul) or by abstracted species (angels are simpler than souls).

### Objection 3
If separated souls understood separate substances, they would achieve ultimate happiness through separation alone.

### On the contrary
The rich man in hell saw Abraham and Lazarus.

### I answer that
The separated soul understands itself through itself. Every separate substance understands what is above and below itself according to its own mode. Since the soul's mode is inferior to angels, it has perfect knowledge of other souls but imperfect knowledge of angels.

### Reply to Objection 1
Though less perfect in nature when separated, the soul gains greater freedom of intelligence, unburdened by the body.

### Reply to Objection 2
The separated soul understands angels through divinely impressed ideas, providing imperfect knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 3
Ultimate happiness consists in knowing God through grace, not in knowing separate substances.

## Article 3: Whether the Separated Soul Understands All Natural Things

### Objection 1
The types of all natural things exist in separate substances. Since separated souls know separate substances, they know all natural things.

### Objection 2
Understanding greater intelligibles enables understanding lesser ones.

### On the contrary
Devils possess greater natural knowledge yet do not know all natural things, learning through long experience.

### I answer that
The separated soul receives only general and confused knowledge through species from Divine light, whereas angels achieve perfect knowledge. Separated souls know all natural things in a general and confused manner, not with certain and proper knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 1
Knowing separate substances does not necessarily yield knowledge of all natural things.

### Reply to Objection 2
The separated soul understands separate substances imperfectly, so neither does it know all natural things perfectly.

## Article 4: Whether the Separated Soul Understands Singulars

### Objection 1
Only the intellect remains in the separated soul. The intellect cannot know singulars.

### Objection 2
The separated soul lacks determinate knowledge of species, so it cannot know singulars.

### Objection 3
If it knew some singulars non-sensually, the same reasoning would apply to all.

### On the contrary
The rich man said: "I have five brethren," indicating knowledge of specific individuals.

### I answer that
Separated souls know some singulars but not all. God knows all singulars through His essence as cause of universal and individual principles. Angels achieve perfect singular knowledge. Separated souls know only those singulars to which they are determined by prior knowledge, affection, natural aptitude, or Divine disposition.

### Reply to Objection 1
The intellect does not know singulars through abstraction; the separated soul knows them through divinely impressed species.

### Reply to Objection 2
Knowledge is confined to species or individuals to which the soul bears determined relation.

### Reply to Objection 3
The soul has different relations to different singulars.

## Article 5: Whether Habits of Knowledge Acquired in This Life Remain

### Objection 1
The Apostle states: "Knowledge shall be destroyed."

### Objection 2
Lesser individuals would exceed better ones in knowledge.

### Objection 3
Two forms of the same species would coexist in one subject.

### Objection 4
Sickness can destroy knowledge; death produces greater change.

### On the contrary
Jerome states: "Let us learn on earth that kind of knowledge which will remain with us in heaven."

### I answer that
Knowledge resides partly in sensitive powers and partly in the intellect. Knowledge regarding sensitive powers does not remain, but regarding the intellect it must remain. The intellect is incorruptible, and intelligible species in the passive intellect lack contraries. Therefore, the habit of knowledge insofar as it exists in the intellect remains.

### Reply to Objection 1
The Apostle speaks of knowledge as an act of knowing, not as a habit.

### Reply to Objection 2
A less good individual may possess knowledge habits the better individual lacks. Such knowledge cannot compare with other prerogatives.

### Reply to Objection 3
These two types of knowledge are not the same species.

### Reply to Objection 4
This objection concerns knowledge corruption through sensitive powers.

## Article 6: Whether the Separated Soul Can Use Acquired Knowledge

### Objection 1
When the body is corrupted, the soul neither remembers nor loves.

### Objection 2
Intelligible species cannot be understood without turning to phantasms.

### Objection 3
Knowledge habits produce acts similar to those acquiring them.

### On the contrary
Christ told the rich man: "Remember thou didst receive good things in thy lifetime."

### I answer that
Since intelligible species remain in the separated soul and its state differs from embodied life, the soul can understand what it formerly understood but in different mode—not by turning to phantasms but by a mode suited to separated existence.

### Reply to Objection 1
The Philosopher speaks of memory as belonging to the sensitive part.

### Reply to Objection 2
The different mode of understanding results from the soul's different state, not from diverse species.

### Reply to Objection 3
Acts producing habits resemble acts caused by those habits in species but not in mode.

## Article 7: Whether Local Distance Impedes the Separated Soul's Knowledge

### Objection 1
Augustine says souls of the dead cannot know what is done here.

### Objection 2
Demonic speed enables revealing unknown things, suggesting distance impedes.

### Objection 3
As place creates distance, so does time. Time-distance impedes the separated soul.

### On the contrary
Dives saw Abraham afar off.

### I answer that
The separated soul understands singulars through species derived from Divine light, which is indifferent to proximity or distance. Therefore, local distance does not hinder separated soul knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 1
Augustine means separated souls cannot see earthly affairs for other causes, not spatial distance.

### Reply to Objection 2
Augustine discusses demons naturally united to bodies, possessing sensitive powers requiring local distance.

### Reply to Objection 3
The future does not actually exist and is unknowable in itself. What is locally distant actually exists. Temporal and spatial distance differ.

## Article 8: Whether Separated Souls Know What Happens Here

### Objection 1
Dives sought to warn his brothers, showing care for the living.

### Objection 2
The dead appear to the living revealing earthly information.

### Objection 3
Separated souls know what happens among themselves.

### On the contrary
Scripture states man "will not understand whether his children come to honor or dishonor."

### I answer that
By natural knowledge, the souls of the dead do not know earthly occurrences. The departed exist in separation through Divine order, joined to the world of incorporeal spiritual substances.

Gregory suggests that blessed souls seeing God know all earthly occurrences. However, being perfectly united to Divine justice, they do not suffer sorrow or interfere except according to Divine justice.

### Reply to Objection 1
Departed souls may care for the living while ignorant of their state. Earthly affairs may become known through newly arriving souls, angels, or Divine revelation.

### Reply to Objection 2
Dead appearing to the living occurs through special Divine dispensation or through angels without the departed's knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 3
Ignorance proceeds not from local distance but from their separated mode of existence.
