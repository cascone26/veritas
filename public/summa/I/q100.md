# Question 100: The Condition of the Offspring as Regards Righteousness

## Article 1: Whether Men Would Have Been Born in a State of Righteousness

### Objection 1
Hugh of St. Victor argues children would be sinless but "not heirs to their father's righteousness."

### Objection 2
Righteousness comes through grace, which God alone infuses—it cannot pass naturally.

### Objection 3
The soul is not transmitted from parent to child, so neither could righteousness.

### On the contrary
Anselm states humans would beget offspring "endowed with righteousness together with the rational soul."

### I answer that
Original righteousness was an accidental quality pertaining to human nature itself—a divine gift to humanity. Since original sin transmits from parents to offspring, children would similarly inherit parental righteousness.

### Reply to Objection 1
Hugh's words concern performing righteous acts, not possessing the virtue.

### Reply to Objection 2
Children would be born in grace through immediate divine infusion upon receiving a rational soul.

### Reply to Objection 3
This follows from the preceding answer.

## Article 2: Whether Children Would Have Been Born Confirmed in Righteousness

### Objection 1
No sinful corruption would produce "children of hell," implying all descendants would be saved.

### Objection 2
Anselm claims persevering Adam would be confirmed, making offspring confirmed too.

### Objection 3
If sin follows from Adam's transgression, righteousness would follow perseverance.

### Objection 4
Angels were confirmed in grace; humans would similarly beget confirmed children.

### On the contrary
Augustine indicates any descendant could commit evil—children wouldn't be born confirmed.

### I answer that
Children would not have been born confirmed. Parents begetting children would not yet possess confirmation. Rational creatures achieve confirmation through the beatific vision of God. Adam would eventually reach this state, becoming wholly spiritual and ending generation. Therefore, confirmed children could never exist.

### Reply to Objection 1
Without sin, children wouldn't inherit damnation, yet could freely choose evil.

### Reply to Objection 2
Anselm offered opinion, not assertion.

### Reply to Objection 3
Transmitted necessity differs from blessed confirmation.

### Reply to Objection 4
Human free will differs fundamentally from angelic will.
