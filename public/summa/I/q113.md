# Question 113: The Guardianship of the Good Angels

## Article 1: Whether Men Are Guarded by the Angels

### Objection 1

Humans can guard themselves through free will and knowledge of natural law. Therefore, angels do not guard men.

### Objection 2

God guards men according to Psalm 120:4. A stronger guard makes a weaker one superfluous. Thus men do not need angelic guards.

### Objection 3

Many fall into sin daily; angels could prevent this through visible appearance or miracles. Therefore angels would be negligent if responsible for guardianship.

### On the contrary

Psalm 90:11 states: "He hath given His angels charge over thee, to keep thee in all thy ways."

### I answer that

According to Divine Providence, movable and variable things are regulated by immovable and invariable things. Human knowledge and affection regarding actions can vary and fail toward good in many ways. Therefore, angels were necessarily deputed to guard men, regulating and moving them toward good.

### Reply to Objection 1

Free will cannot sufficiently avoid evil due to weakness from manifold passions. Universal natural knowledge directs men insufficiently toward particular actions. Thus man needs angelic guardians.

### Reply to Objection 2

God guards men immediately by infusing grace and virtues. Regarding reason, God instructs through angels as intermediaries.

### Reply to Objection 3

Men's perdition results from human malice, not angelic negligence. Visible appearances and miracles come from special Divine grace.

## Article 2: Whether Each Man Is Guarded by an Angel

### Objection 1

One man can guard many. Therefore one angel can guard many men.

### Objection 2

Only one angel stands between the highest and men. Therefore only one angel guards men.

### Objection 3

Guarding one man versus another is not a greater office. Thus different men need not be guarded by different angels.

### On the contrary

Jerome states: "Great is the dignity of souls, for each one to have an angel deputed to guard it from its birth."

### I answer that

Each man has an angel guardian. God's providence toward each man is like providence toward every species of corruptible things, since men are incorruptible in their individual rational souls. Gregory notes different orders are deputed to different genera. Similarly, different angels are appointed to different men.

### Reply to Objection 1

Angelic guardians are given regarding individual salvation's invisible things. Thus individual angels guard individual men.

### Reply to Objection 2

One angel can enlighten a man immediately while enlightening other angels beneath him. Lower angels are enlightened by their immediate superior in some matters.

### Reply to Objection 3

Although men are equal in nature, inequality exists according to Divine Providence ordering some to greater things. Thus guarding one man can be a greater office than guarding another.

## Article 3: Whether Guardianship Belongs Only to the Lowest Order of Angels

### Objection 1

Chrysostom says Matthew 18:10 refers to the highest angels. Therefore superior angels guard men.

### Objection 2

Five orders are sent in external ministry. Therefore all five are deputed to human guardianship.

### Objection 3

Guardianship requires coercing demons and working miracles. Therefore Powers and Virtues are also deputed.

### On the contrary

Psalm 90 attributes guardianship to angels, who belong to the lowest order according to Dionysius.

### I answer that

Man is guarded in two ways: particular guardianship (one angel per man, belonging to the lowest order) and universal guardianship (multiplied according to different orders, with more universal agents being higher). Michael, called an archangel, is also styled "one of the princes" (Daniel 10:13). All corporeal creatures are guarded by Virtues; demons by Powers; good spirits by Principalities.

### Reply to Objection 1

Chrysostom means the highest among the lowest order. Probably, greater angels guard those chosen for higher glory.

### Reply to Objection 2

Not all sent angels have guardianship of individual men; some orders have universal guardianship.

### Reply to Objection 3

All lowest-order angels can coerce demons and work miracles by sharing in superior orders' gifts.

## Article 4: Whether Angels Are Appointed to Guardianship of All Men

### Objection 1

Christ would have had a guardian, which is unseemly since Christ is greater than all angels.

### Objection 2

Adam in innocence faced no dangers, so an angel guardian would be unfitting.

### Objection 3

The damned never attain eternal life; infidels lack right intention; Antichrist acts according to Satan. Therefore angels are not deputed to all.

### On the contrary

Jerome states: "Each soul has an angel appointed to guard it."

### I answer that

Man in this life journeys toward heaven on a dangerous road. Therefore an angel guardian is assigned to each man while he is a wayfarer. Upon reaching life's end, he no longer has a guardian angel; in the kingdom, an angel reigns with him; in hell, a demon punishes him.

### Reply to Objection 1

Christ as man was guided immediately by the Word of God. He had not a superior guardian but a ministering inferior angel (Matthew 4:11).

### Reply to Objection 2

In innocence, man faced no interior peril but exterior danger from demons' snares, as events proved. Therefore he needed a guardian angel.

### Reply to Objection 3

The foreknown, infidels, and Antichrist are not deprived of angelic guardianship extended to the human race. Demons are restrained by good angels lest they harm more.

## Article 5: Whether an Angel Is Appointed to Guard a Man from His Birth

### Objection 1

Angels minister for those receiving salvation's inheritance. Men begin receiving this at baptism, not birth.

### Objection 2

Children lack reason-use after birth. Therefore angels are not appointed at birth.

### Objection 3

A child possesses a rational soul before birth. It is not apparent an angel guards before birth.

### On the contrary

Jerome states: "Each soul has an angel appointed to guard it from its birth."

### I answer that

Benefits conferred as Christian begin at baptism. Benefits conferred as a rational being are bestowed at birth. Angelic guardianship belongs to the latter. Therefore, from birth, man has an angel guardian.

### Reply to Objection 1

Angels minister efficaciously for those receiving salvation ultimately, but their ministrations extend to others, warding off evils.

### Reply to Objection 2

Guardianship's ultimate effect is instruction, yet it includes warding off demons and preventing bodily and spiritual harm in childhood.

### Reply to Objection 3

While in the womb, the child is part of the mother. The mother's guardian angel guards the child. At birth, becoming separate, an angel guardian is appointed.

## Article 6: Whether the Angel Guardian Ever Forsakes a Man

### Objection 1

Jeremiah 51:9 states: "Let us forsake her." Isaiah 5:5 says: "I will take away the hedge"—the guardianship of angels.

### Objection 2

God forsakes man at times (Psalm 21:2). Therefore an angel guardian much more forsakes man.

### Objection 3

Angels are sometimes in heaven, not here with us. Therefore sometimes they forsake us.

### On the contrary

Demons constantly assail us (1 Peter 5:8). Good angels much more ever guard us.

### I answer that

The angel guardian never entirely forsakes man but sometimes leaves him in particulars—not preventing subjection to trouble or even sin, according to Divine judgments' ordering.

### Reply to Objection 1

Addressed in the main answer.

### Reply to Objection 2

Addressed in the main answer.

### Reply to Objection 3

Though an angel may locally forsake a man, he does not forsake regarding guardianship effects: even in heaven, he knows man's situation and can be with man instantly.

## Article 7: Whether Angels Grieve for the Ills of Those They Guard

### Objection 1

Isaiah 33:7 states: "The angels of peace shall weep bitterly."

### Objection 2

Loss of the guarded man is against the guardian angel's will. Therefore angels grieve.

### Objection 3

Angels rejoice when one sinner repents (Luke 15:7). Therefore they grieve when a just man falls into sin.

### Objection 4

Angels are judged as to whether men fell through negligence or their own fault.

### On the contrary

Where grief exists, perfect happiness is absent. Angels are perfectly happy.

### I answer that

Angels do not grieve for sins or pains inflicted on men. Nothing happens in the world contrary to blessed angels' will because their will cleaves entirely to Divine justice's ordering. Though angels do not will sin universally, they do will the fulfillment of Divine justice regarding such matters.

### Reply to Objection 1

"Angels of peace" may be apostles and preachers. If understood of blessed angels, the expression is metaphorical.

### Reply to Objection 2

Apparent from the answer.

### Reply to Objection 3

Both in man's repentance and sin is one reason for joy: the fulfillment of Divine Providence's ordering.

### Reply to Objection 4

Angels are judged not as guilty but as witnesses to convict man of weakness.

## Article 8: Whether Strife or Discord Exists Among Angels

### Objection 1

Job 25:2 states: "Who maketh peace in His high places."

### Objection 2

Perfect charity and just authority preclude strife.

### Objection 3

If angels strive, one takes one side, another the opposite—one would compound wrong.

### On the contrary

Daniel 10:13 states: "The prince of the kingdom of the Persians resisted me one and twenty days."

### I answer that

Gregory states Persia's prince was a good angel appointed to that kingdom's guardianship. Angels consulting concerning contrary, opposing merits are said to resist one another: not that their wills oppose, since all agree on Divine decree's fulfillment, but the things about which they seek knowledge are in opposition. Kingdoms and men have contrary merits and demerits; angels must consult Divine wisdom regarding these.
