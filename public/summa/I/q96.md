# Question 96: The Mastership Belonging to Man in the State of Innocence

## Article 1: Whether Adam Had Mastership Over Animals

### Objection 1
Augustine states that angels directed animals to Adam for naming, suggesting man lacked independent mastery.

### Objection 2
Hostile animals cannot logically fall under one master's authority.

### Objection 3
God gave man mastership although before sin he had no need of animals.

### Objection 4
Commands apply only to rational beings.

### On the contrary
Genesis declares man should have "dominion over the fishes of the sea, and the birds of the air, and the beasts of the earth."

### I answer that
Man was punished through animal disobedience after sin; in innocence, all naturally subject creatures obeyed him. Three proofs: natural order (lower serves higher), Divine Providence (man governs animals as God's image), and reason's superiority over animal instinct.

### Reply to Objection 1
Angels could perform feats impossible for man, yet man retained mastery.

### Reply to Objection 2
Predatory animals retained their natures, but man's mastery encompassed all creatures through God's Providence.

### Reply to Objection 3
Man needed animals for experiential knowledge of their natures.

### Reply to Objection 4
Animals possess natural prudence enabling obedience through instinct.

## Article 2: Whether Man Had Mastership Over All Creatures

### Objection 1
Angels exceed man in power.

### Objection 2
Plant powers naturally disobey reason.

### Objection 3
Only God can alter celestial bodies' courses.

### On the contrary
Genesis states man would have dominion "over every creature."

### I answer that
Man had no mastership over angels. Over animals, mastery existed through commanding obedience. Over plants and inanimate things, mastery existed through use, not command or alteration.

## Article 3: Whether Men Were Equal in the State of Innocence

### Objection 1
Gregory teaches "where there is no sin, there is no inequality."

### Objection 2
Innocent society's love required equality.

### Objection 3
Inequality stems from punishment and defects absent in innocence.

### On the contrary
Order requires proper placement of equal and unequal things.

### I answer that
Natural inequalities would have existed in sex, age, soul (through free-will application), and body (through climate and stellar influences)—without defect.

### Reply to Objection 1
Gregory excludes only inequality between virtue and vice.

### Reply to Objection 2
Greater love can exist between unequals.

### Reply to Objection 3
God could elevate some above others without punitive subjection.

## Article 4: Whether Man Would Have Been Master Over Man

### Objection 1
Augustine states God willed man to "rule over none but irrational creatures."

### Objection 2
Subjection originated as sin's penalty.

### Objection 3
Liberty constitutes chief blessing.

### On the contrary
Angels possess rulership without degradation.

### I answer that
Slavish mastery (subject referred to master's benefit) was impossible in innocence. Governance (directing free subjects toward their good) would have existed. Community requires direction toward common good. Superior virtue and knowledge must benefit others.

### Reply to Objection 1
Augustine refers to slavish dominion, not governance.

### Reply to Objection 2
Same clarification applies.

### Reply to Objection 3
Governance through counsel doesn't destroy liberty.
