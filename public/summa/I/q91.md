# Question 91: The Production of the First Man's Body

## Article 1: Whether the Body of the First Man Was Made of the Slime of the Earth

### Objection 1
Creating something from nothing demonstrates greater divine power. Man's body should showcase this maximally, warranting creation ex nihilo rather than from earthly slime.

### Objection 2
Heavenly bodies surpass earthly ones in nobility. The human body should derive from celestial rather than terrestrial matter.

### Objection 3
Fire and air possess greater nobility than earth and water. Man's dignity demands composition from fire and air.

### Objection 4
Since the human body comprises four elements, it wasn't formed exclusively from earthly slime.

### On the contrary
"God made man of the slime of the earth" (Genesis 2:7).

### I answer that
God perfects all works according to their capacity. Man represents a microcosm—composed of all creatures. His rational soul belongs to spiritual substances; his temperament resembles heavenly bodies' equability; yet he contains all four elements. The higher elements (fire and air) predominate through power while inferior elements abound quantitatively, ensuring balanced mixing. "Slime" (earth and water combined) appropriately describes man's composition.

### Reply to Objection 1
The four-element composition fittingly positioned man between spiritual and corporeal substances.

### Reply to Objection 2
Heavenly bodies suit the rational soul poorly. The rational soul acquires knowledge through sensation, requiring passible sense organs impossible in impassible heavenly matter.

### Reply to Objection 3
If fire and air predominated quantitatively, they would absorb remaining elements, destroying the elemental equality necessary for touch-sense.

### Reply to Objection 4
Scripture omits fire and air because they exist in lesser quantity and escape uncultured perception.

## Article 2: Whether the Human Body Was Immediately Produced by God

### Objection 1
Augustine teaches that God disposes corporeal things through angels. Angels likely mediated body production.

### Objection 2
Created powers suffice for effects they can produce. Heavenly bodies generate certain animals from putrefaction.

### Objection 3
Corporeal matter undergoes change solely through heavenly body movement.

### Objection 4
Augustine states man's body pre-existed through causal virtues in corporeal creatures.

### On the contrary
"God created man out of the earth" (Sirach 17:1).

### I answer that
The first human body formation required God's immediate action. Forms only occur in composites; the agent must resemble its effect. God alone, though immaterial, creates matter itself. Angels cannot transform bodies except using seed-nature materials. Since no prior human body existed, God's immediate production was necessary.

### Reply to Objection 1
God performs actions exceeding angelic power. Angels could assist ministerially but not produce the body.

### Reply to Objection 2
Perfect animals cannot result from heavenly body power alone. Heavenly bodies suffice only for imperfect animals from properly disposed matter.

### Reply to Objection 3
Man's formation from earth-slime parallels supernatural effects exceeding nature's ordinary course.

### Reply to Objection 4
The human body pre-existed in passive potentiality only—producible from pre-existing matter by God alone.

## Article 3: Whether the Body of Man Was Given an Apt Disposition

### Objection 1
Man surpasses all animals, yet dogs smell keener and birds fly swifter. Man's body lacks apt disposition.

### Objection 2
Other animals possess natural covering and defensive arms; man lacks these.

### Objection 3
Plants grow erect; brutes prone. Man should not possess erect stature if he stands further from plants than from brutes.

### On the contrary
"God made man right" (Ecclesiastes 7:30).

### I answer that
God fashioned the human body with disposition most suiting the rational soul and its operations. Apparent defects necessarily arise from material conditions required for soul-body proportioning.

### Reply to Objection 1
Touch-sense achieves greater perfection in man. Man excels in interior sensitive powers. He requires the largest brain relative to body-size for intellectual freedom. Brain humidity impedes smell. Some animals see or hear keener due to necessary hindrance from human temperamental balance.

### Reply to Objection 2
Horns, claws, thick hides indicate earthly-element abundance incompatible with human equability. Man possesses reason and hands—"the organ of organs"—creating infinite instrument varieties. This befits rational nature conceiving infinite things.

### Reply to Objection 3
Erect stature suits man for four reasons: senses serve knowledge beyond survival; erect posture frees interior powers; hands serve purposes beyond locomotion; prone stature would force mouth-grasping, preventing speech.

## Article 4: Whether the Production of the Human Body Is Fittingly Described in Scripture

### Objection 1
Other works receive "God said, let it be made." Man should receive identical description.

### Objection 2
"Let us make man" suggests mediated production.

### Objection 3
After "God made man from earth-slime," adding "He breathed into him the breath of life" seems redundant.

### Objection 4
Scripture's statement "He breathed into his face" appears unfitting since life-breath permeates the entire body.

### Objection 5
After "To His image He made them," adding "male and female He created them" seems incongruous.

### On the contrary
Scripture's authority suffices.

### I answer that
Scripture uses special language for man's production, signifying that other creatures exist for man's sake. "Let us make man" signifies divine-person plurality, whose image expresses itself more clearly in man.

### Reply to Objection 1
Man surpasses other things in bearing God's image. We deliberate more carefully regarding prioritized concerns.

### Reply to Objection 2
These words signify divine-person plurality, not angelic cooperation.

### Reply to Objection 3
"God made man" denotes body-soul production together. "He breathed into his face the breath of life" signifies the soul—the body's form.

### Reply to Objection 4
Vital operations manifest visibly in man's face through sense-expressions.

### Reply to Objection 5
Both body and soul were made on the sixth day.
