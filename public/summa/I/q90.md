# Question 90: The First Production of Man's Soul

## Article 1: Whether the Soul Was Made or Was of God's Substance?

### Objection 1
Genesis 2:7 states God "breathed into his face the breath of life." Since one who breathes sends forth something of themselves, the soul must be of the Divine substance.

### Objection 2
The soul is a simple form and thus pure act. Only God is pure act, so the soul must be God's substance.

### Objection 3
Things that exist but differ must be identical, as difference requires composition. God and the human mind both exist without difference.

### On the contrary
Augustine condemns as "exceedingly and evidently perverse" the opinion that "God made the soul not out of nothing, but from Himself."

### I answer that
The claim that the soul is God's substance is manifestly impossible. The human soul exists in potentiality regarding intelligence, acquires knowledge from things, and possesses various powers—all incompatible with God's nature as pure act.

This error originated from ancient philosophers who supposed God was corporeal and the soul shared that nature. The Manichaeans held the soul was part of divine light. Later thinkers conceived God as an incorporeal world-soul. All such theories are impossible.

### Reply to Objection 1
"Breathe" should not be understood materially. For God, breathing means creating spirit.

### Reply to Objection 2
Though the soul is simple in essence, it is not its own existence but participates in being. Therefore it is not pure act like God.

### Reply to Objection 3
Simple substances are diverse without differing by composition, as a horse and human differ by rational/irrational without those properties differing further.

## Article 2: Whether the Soul Was Produced by Creation?

### Objection 1
Whatever contains something material is produced from matter. The soul is not pure act.

### Objection 2
The soul is the act of corporeal matter, so it is educed from matter's potential.

### Objection 3
If the soul is created, all forms would be created, meaning nothing comes through generation.

### On the contrary
Genesis 1:27 states "God created man to His own image." Man resembles God especially through the soul.

### I answer that
Only the rational soul can be produced by creation. Since becoming is the path to existence, things must be made according to their mode of existing. Non-subsistent forms arise when composite substances are made. The rational soul, being subsistent, cannot originate from pre-existing matter and cannot exist except through creation.

### Reply to Objection 1
The soul's simple essence functions as material element, while its participated existence functions as formal element.

### Reply to Objection 2
The rational soul, being subsistent and exceeding corporeal matter's capacity, is not educed from matter's potentiality.

### Reply to Objection 3
The rational soul differs fundamentally from other forms.

## Article 3: Whether the Rational Soul Is Produced by God Immediately?

### Objection 1
Inferior bodies are produced through superior ones. Therefore, rational souls should be produced through angels.

### Objection 2
Inferior things are forwarded by the higher. Souls should be produced by higher beings.

### Objection 3
Spiritual substances are more perfect than corporeal ones. Since bodies produce their species, angels should produce something inferior like the rational soul.

### On the contrary
Genesis 2:7 states God Himself "breathed into the face of man the breath of life."

### I answer that
The rational soul can only be produced by creation. God alone can create, as the first agent acts without presupposing existence. Since the rational soul cannot be produced by transmuting matter, it cannot exist save immediately through God.

## Article 4: Whether the Human Soul Was Produced Before the Body?

### Objection 1
Creation preceded distinction and adornment. The soul was created while the body was formed later.

### Objection 2
The rational soul shares more with angels than with animals. Angels were created before bodies.

### Objection 3
The soul outlasts the body at the end, so it was created before the body at the beginning.

### On the contrary
The proper act is produced in its proper potentiality. The soul is the body's proper act.

### I answer that
Origen held all souls were created simultaneously before bodies. Augustine suggested the first man's soul was created with the angels. However, if the soul is the body's form and naturally part of human nature, it achieves natural perfection only united to the body. Creating the soul separately would be unfitting.

### Reply to Objection 1
If the soul were a complete species creatable independently, this would prove separate creation. But as the body's natural form, it was necessarily created in the body.

### Reply to Objection 2
As the body's form, the soul belongs to the animal genus as its formal principle.

### Reply to Objection 3
The soul's remaining after bodily death results from death itself—a defect absent when the soul was first created.
