# Question 106: How One Creature Moves Another

## Article 1: Whether One Angel Enlightens Another

### Objection 1

Angels possess the same beatitude we hope to obtain. But humans will not teach one another in heaven, according to Jeremiah 31:34: "They shall teach no more every man his neighbor." Therefore, angels do not enlighten one another now.

### Objection 2

Light in angels is threefold: of nature, grace, and glory. An angel receives enlightenment in each from God alone. Therefore, one angel does not enlighten another.

### Objection 3

Light is a form in the mind. Augustine states the rational mind is "informed by God alone, without created intervention." Therefore, one angel does not enlighten another's mind.

### On the contrary

Dionysius affirms that "the angels of the second hierarchy are cleansed, enlightened and perfected by the angels of the first hierarchy" (Coel. Hier. viii).

### I answer that

One angel does enlighten another. Intellectual light is nothing but manifestation of truth, as stated in Ephesians 5:13: "All that is made manifest is light." To enlighten means communicating to others the manifestation of known truth.

Since two things concur in intellectual operation—the intellectual power and the likeness of the thing understood—one angel notifies known truth to another in both respects. First, by strengthening the intellectual power: just as imperfect bodies are strengthened by proximity to more perfect ones, the intellectual power of an inferior angel is strengthened when the superior angel turns toward him. Second, one angel manifests truth regarding the likeness of the thing understood: the superior angel receives knowledge through universal conception, which the inferior angel's intellect cannot sufficiently grasp. The superior angel therefore distinguishes universally conceived truth so it can be grasped by the inferior, as Dionysius states (Coel. Hier. xv).

### Reply to Objection 1

All angels see God's Essence immediately, and in this respect one does not teach another. However, concerning types of Divine works—known in God as their cause—a superior angel knows more types the more perfectly he sees God. The superior angel thus enlightens the inferior regarding these.

### Reply to Objection 2

An angel does not enlighten another by giving the light of nature, grace, or glory itself, but by strengthening his natural light and manifesting truth concerning the state of nature, grace, and glory.

### Reply to Objection 3

The rational mind is formed immediately by God as image from exemplar or as subject by perfecting form. Other enlightenment from human or angelic sources are dispositions to this ultimate form.

## Article 2: Whether One Angel Moves Another Angel's Will

### Objection 1

As one angel enlightens another, so he cleanses and perfects another. Cleansing and perfecting belong to the will. Therefore, an angel can move another's will.

### Objection 2

Dionysius states the Seraphim "kindle" or "give heat"—acts of love belonging to the will. Therefore, one angel moves another's will.

### Objection 3

The Philosopher states the higher appetite moves the lower. As the superior angel's intellect is higher, so is his will. Therefore the superior angel can change another's will.

### On the contrary

To change the will belongs to whoever bestows righteousness. God alone bestows righteousness. Therefore, one angel cannot change another's will.

### I answer that

The will changes in two ways: regarding the object and regarding the power. Regarding the object, nothing sufficiently moves the will except the universal good, which is God alone. An angel inclines the will as something lovable and by manifesting created good ordered to God's goodness, thus inclining it through persuasion.

Regarding the power itself, only God can move the will. The will's operation is an inclination of the willer to the willed. Only He who bestowed the power to will can change this inclination. Since God alone authored intellectual nature, only He can change the will's natural inclination.

### Reply to Objection 1

Cleansing and perfecting follow the mode of enlightenment. An angel cleanses from nescience in the intellect and perfects toward the intellect's end—knowledge of truth, as Dionysius states (Eccl. Hier. vi).

### Reply to Objection 2

One angel can induce another to love God through persuasion.

### Reply to Objection 3

The Philosopher discusses the lower sensitive appetite moved by the superior intellectual appetite within the same soul-nature. This does not apply to angels.

## Article 3: Whether an Inferior Angel Can Enlighten a Superior Angel

### Objection 1

In the Church, even superiors are enlightened by inferiors, as the Apostle states: "You may all prophesy one by one, that all may learn" (1 Corinthians 14:31). Therefore, superiors can be enlightened by inferiors in the heavenly hierarchy.

### Objection 2

God sometimes acts outside the order of corporeal substances. Therefore, He also acts outside the order of spiritual substances, enlightening inferiors directly, who can then enlighten superiors.

### Objection 3

One angel enlightens another by turning to him voluntarily. The highest angel can turn to the lowest, so inferiors enlightened by God could enlighten superiors.

### On the contrary

Dionysius states: "This is the Divine unalterable law, that inferior things are led to God by the superior" (Coel. Hier. iv).

### I answer that

Inferior angels never enlighten superiors but are always enlightened by them. God works miraculously outside corporeal nature's order so men are directed to knowledge of Him. However, passing over the spiritual substances' order does not serve man's ordering to God, since angelic operations are not made known to us as sensible operations are. Thus God never passes over the order of spiritual substances.

### Reply to Objection 1

In the ecclesiastical hierarchy, those nearest to God in sanctity sometimes occupy the lowest grade. Therefore, superiors may be taught by inferiors there. In the heavenly hierarchy, those nearer God are always more sublime in grade and clearer in knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 2

There is dissimilarity between what God does outside corporeal nature's order and spiritual nature's order.

### Reply to Objection 3

An angel voluntarily turns to enlighten another, but the angel's will is ever regulated by the Divine law establishing the angels' order.

## Article 4: Whether the Superior Angel Enlightens the Inferior Regarding All He Himself Knows

### Objection 1

Superior angels have more universal knowledge; inferiors have more particular knowledge. More is contained under universal knowledge. Therefore, not all the superior knows is known by the inferior through enlightenment.

### Objection 2

Superior angels long knew the Mystery of the Incarnation, whereas inferior angels did not know it until accomplished. Therefore, superiors do not enlighten inferiors about all they know.

### Objection 3

If superiors enlighten inferiors about all they know, nothing superiors know would remain unknown to inferiors. Therefore superiors could communicate nothing more.

### On the contrary

Gregory states: "In that heavenly country, nothing is held individually." Dionysius affirms: "Each heavenly essence communicates to the inferior the gift derived from the superior" (Coel. Hier. xv).

### I answer that

Every creature participates in Divine goodness so as to diffuse the good it possesses to others. The more an agent shares in Divine goodness, the more it transmits its perfections. Therefore, the holy angels impart the same to those below them.

Nevertheless, the inferior do not receive this gift as excellently as superiors; the superior thus remain in higher order with more perfect knowledge, as the master understands better than the pupil.

### Reply to Objection 1

Superior angels' knowledge is more universal regarding the more eminent mode of knowledge.

### Reply to Objection 2

Inferiors were not entirely ignorant of the Incarnation, but did not know it as fully as superiors and progressed in knowledge when the Mystery was accomplished.

### Reply to Objection 3

Until the Judgment Day, God reveals new things to the highest angels concerning the world's course. Thus superiors always have something to make known to inferiors.
