# Question 3: What is Happiness

## Article 1: Whether Happiness is Something Uncreated

### Objection 1
Boethius states that "God is happiness itself," suggesting happiness is uncreated.

### Objection 2
Since happiness represents the supreme good and only God is the supreme good, happiness must be identical with God.

### Objection 3
Man's will naturally tends toward God alone as the ultimate end to be enjoyed, making happiness the same as God.

### On the Contrary
Augustine teaches that things making us happy are created. Therefore, happiness itself must be created rather than uncreated.

### I Answer That
Two aspects of ends exist: the thing desired itself, and the attainment or enjoyment of that thing. God represents man's final end in the first sense—the uncreated good satisfying the will. However, in the second sense, happiness consists in the created possession or enjoyment existing within man. Thus happiness is uncreated regarding its cause, yet created regarding its essence.

### Reply to Objection 1
God possesses happiness by His essence, while humans achieve it through participation. This participation is itself something created.

### Reply to Objection 2
Happiness represents man's supreme good as the attainment of the supreme good rather than being identical with it.

### Reply to Objection 3
Happiness functions as the final end in the same way that attaining an end constitutes the end itself.

---

## Article 2: Whether Happiness is an Operation

### Objection 1
Scripture presents eternal life as the final end, yet life represents being rather than operation.

### Objection 2
Boethius defines happiness as "a state made perfect by the aggregate of all good things," not an operation.

### Objection 3
Happiness signifies something existing in the happy person rather than something proceeding from them, contrary to operation's nature.

### Objection 4
Happiness remains continuously in the happy person, whereas operations pass away temporarily.

### Objection 5
Unity characterizes happiness, but multiple operations exist, so happiness cannot be operational.

### Objection 6
Humans experience interrupted operations through sleep and occupation, yet happiness should remain uninterrupted.

### On the Contrary
Aristotle declares that "happiness is an operation according to perfect virtue."

### I Answer That
Man's created happiness necessarily consists in operation. Perfection exists through actuality, not mere potentiality. Operation represents the ultimate actualization of the operator. Therefore, man's happiness must consist in his final act, which is operation.

### Reply to Objection 1
Life signifies two things: the being of living things, or the operation through which life's principle becomes actual. Eternal life means the operation whereby man knows God, as stated in John 17:3.

### Reply to Objection 2
Boethius considered happiness generally as a perfect state, while Aristotle expressed its essential nature as the operation establishing this state.

### Reply to Objection 3
Two types of action exist: those proceeding outward affecting external matter, and those remaining in the agent as perfection. Only the latter can constitute happiness.

### Reply to Objection 4
Perfect happiness appears in God, whose being is His operation. In angels, a single eternal operation unites them to God. Currently in humans, the operation uniting us to God cannot be continuous, preventing perfect present happiness. However, in the future heavenly state, continuous union with God will constitute perfect happiness. Present contemplative life approaches this ideal more than active life.

## Article 3: Whether Happiness is an Operation of the Sensitive or Intellectual Part Only

### Objection 1
Intellective operations depend on sensation, since understanding requires sensory images.

### Objection 2
Happiness as perfect good requires sensible goods, demanding sensory operations.

### Objection 3
Perfect happiness should perfect man entirely, including parts perfected by sensation.

### On the Contrary
Irrational animals possess sensation like humans but lack human happiness, proving happiness excludes mere sensory operation.

### I Answer That
Sensory operation belongs to happiness in three ways: essentially, antecedently, or consequently. Essentially, it cannot constitute happiness since man's union with God transcends sensory capacity. However, sensory operations belong antecedently in imperfect earthly happiness and consequently in perfect heavenly happiness when bodily senses receive overflow from the soul's joy.

### Reply to Objection 1
This demonstrates sensory operation's necessity for imperfect earthly happiness only.

### Reply to Objection 2
Perfect happiness, being united to all good's source, includes aggregate goodness without requiring each individual good.

### Reply to Objection 3
In perfect happiness, the lower nature becomes perfected through overflow from the higher nature.

---

## Article 4: Whether Happiness Consists in the Intellect or the Will

### Objection 1
Augustine identifies happiness with peace, which pertains to will.

### Objection 2
The will's proper object is good, and happiness is supreme good, so happiness belongs to the will.

### Objection 3
The will, as first mover of operations, corresponds to the final end.

### Objection 4
Love (will's act) surpasses knowledge (intellect's act) in excellence.

### Objection 5
Augustine states the happy person desires nothing amiss and possesses a good will.

### On the Contrary
Christ declares eternal life consists in knowing the only true God, identifying happiness with intellectual knowledge.

### I Answer That
Happiness's essence cannot consist in will's operation. Attainment of the end differs from desiring it; desire merely moves toward the end. The end's presence results from something other than willing. Just as grasping money makes it present rather than merely desiring it, intellectual operation makes the intelligible end present, afterward producing will's delight. Therefore, happiness's essence involves intellection, while resulting joy pertains to the will.

### Reply to Objection 1
Peace pertains to the final end antecedently by removing obstacles and consequently as the rest following attainment.

### Reply to Objection 2
The will's primary object is not its own operation but the good itself.

### Reply to Objection 3
Intellection precedes the will's movement temporally; will's motion toward ends follows intellectual apprehension.

### Reply to Objection 4
Love surpasses knowledge in moving toward action, yet knowledge precedes in attaining the end.

### Reply to Objection 5
Possessing desired things requires something beyond willing; a good will represents a necessary disposition toward happiness.

## Article 5: Whether Happiness is Speculative or Practical Intellect

### Objection 1
Man mirrors God through practical intellect, which causes understood things, more than speculative intellect.

### Objection 2
Practical intellect relates to good while speculative relates to truth; happiness concerns the good.

### Objection 3
Practical intellect concerns man's operations and character rather than external things.

### On the Contrary
Augustine states that "contemplation is promised us as the goal of all actions."

### I Answer That
Happiness consists primarily in speculative rather than practical intellection. Man's supreme operation involves his highest power upon its highest object. The intellect's highest object is divine good, proper to speculative rather than practical intellect. Contemplation is sought for itself, while practical operations serve external ends. Contemplative life shares something with God and angels absent in practical life, which animals partially share. Perfect future happiness consists entirely in contemplation; imperfect present happiness involves contemplation primarily and practical intellect secondarily.

### Reply to Objection 1
Practical intellect's similarity to God is proportional; speculative intellect's similarity through union is superior, and God's essential self-knowledge is speculative.

### Reply to Objection 2
Speculative intellect possesses intrinsic good—truth's contemplation—perfecting the whole person.

### Reply to Objection 3
Since man's final end lies external to himself in God, speculative intellection directed toward God constitutes happiness more than practical self-direction.

---

## Article 6: Whether Happiness Consists in Speculative Sciences

### Objection 1
Aristotle identifies happiness with operation according to virtue, listing three speculative virtues relating to speculative sciences.

### Objection 2
All humans naturally desire knowledge; speculative sciences are sought inherently for themselves.

### Objection 3
Happiness perfects the intellect by reducing it from potentiality to actuality through speculative sciences.

### On the Contrary
Scripture warns: "Let not the wise man glory in his wisdom," concerning speculative sciences.

### I Answer That
Perfect happiness cannot consist essentially in speculative sciences. Speculative science consideration extends only within its principles' scope; principles derive from sensory knowledge. Therefore, speculative science cannot exceed sensible knowledge's reach. Since man's final perfection transcends sensibles, it cannot consist in speculative sciences. However, speculative sciences represent a participation in true happiness, like sensible forms participating in higher substances.

### Reply to Objection 1
Aristotle addresses imperfect happiness achievable in this life.

### Reply to Objection 2
Perfect happiness, along with any participation therein, is naturally desired.

### Reply to Objection 3
Speculative sciences actualize the intellect incompletely, not to its final perfect state.

---

## Article 7: Whether Happiness Consists in Knowing Angels

### Objection 1
Gregory indicates participating in angels' feasts constitutes final happiness, achieved through contemplating them.

### Objection 2
Each thing perfects when united to its principle; human knowledge originates from angels' illumination.

### Objection 3
Lower natures perfect when united to higher natures, as bodies perfecting through spirit.

### On the Contrary
Scripture states final glory consists in understanding and knowing God alone.

### I Answer That
Perfect happiness consists only in divine vision. The intellect's perfection determines by its object's nature. Truth order matches being's order. Since angels possess participated being, they possess participated truth. Perfect happiness requires contemplating what possesses truth essentially—God alone. However, imperfect happiness might involve angels' contemplation, exceeding speculative science but falling short of perfect happiness.

### Reply to Objection 1
Sharing angels' feasts involves contemplating God together with them, not only them.

### Reply to Objection 2
Human souls are created directly by God, not angels. God, not angels as ministers, represents the soul's first principle and final perfection.

### Reply to Objection 3
Lower natures reach higher natures through participating power or by attaining the object. The intellect's final perfection requires attaining what fully possesses its formal object.

---

## Article 8: Whether Man's Happiness Consists in the Vision of the Divine Essence

### Objection 1
Dionysius teaches man is united to God as something unknown through intellect's highest faculty, contradicting essence vision.

### Objection 2
Self-essence comprehension perfects divine intellect; human intellect cannot reach this perfection.

### On the Contrary
Scripture promises that upon Christ's appearing, "we shall see Him as He is."

### I Answer That
Perfect happiness consists solely in divine essence vision. Two points establish this: perfect happiness requires no remaining desire, and power's perfection matches its object's nature. The intellect's object is essence. Knowing an effect's essence without knowing the cause's essence leaves natural desire for the cause's nature. This desire ceases only in achieving the cause's essence knowledge. Therefore, perfect human happiness requires intellectual union with God's very essence.

### Reply to Objection 1
Dionysius addresses wayfarer knowledge en route to happiness.

### Reply to Objection 2
The final end has twofold meaning: the thing itself and its attainment. God remains the end for all things; the attainment mode differs by nature. God's happiness in comprehending His essence surpasses human and angelic happiness in merely beholding It.
