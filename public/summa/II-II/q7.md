# Question 7: The Effects of Faith

## Article 1: Whether Fear is an Effect of Faith?

### Objection 1
Effects cannot precede their causes. Fear precedes faith, as written: "Ye that fear the Lord, believe in Him" (Sirach 2:8). Therefore, fear is not an effect of faith.

### Objection 2
The same thing cannot cause contraries. Fear and hope are contrary; faith begets hope yet would beget fear. Therefore, fear cannot be an effect of faith.

### Objection 3
Contraries do not cause one another. Faith's object is good (First Truth); fear's object is evil. Since acts take their nature from objects, faith cannot cause fear.

### On the Contrary
Scripture states: "The devils...believe and tremble" (James 2:19).

### I Answer That
Fear belongs to appetitive power, moved by apprehended good or evil. Through faith, we apprehend penal evils inflicted by Divine judgment. Thus faith causes servile fear (dread of punishment) and filial fear (dread of separation from God, reverence toward Him). Lifeless faith causes servile fear; living faith quickened by charity causes filial fear.

### Reply to Objection 1
Fear of God requires some faith regarding rewards and punishments. Initial reverential fear from appreciating Divine excellence then moves one to submit intellect to God, enabling belief in all Divine promises.

### Reply to Objection 2
Faith causes contraries under different aspects: it begets hope regarding Divine rewards while causing fear regarding Divine punishments inflicted on sinners.

### Reply to Objection 3
Though faith's formal object is the supreme good, its material object includes certain evils—separation from God, or penal suffering. Faith thus causes fear through this material dimension.

## Article 2: Whether Faith Purifies the Heart?

### Objection 1
Heart purity pertains to affections, while faith resides in intellect. Therefore, faith does not purify the heart.

### Objection 2
What purifies is incompatible with impurity. Faith coexists with sin's impurity in those possessing lifeless faith. Therefore, faith does not purify hearts.

### Objection 3
If faith purified anything, it would purify intellect from obscurity. Yet faith is veiled knowledge. Therefore, faith cannot purify the heart.

### On the Contrary
Peter declared: "Purifying their hearts by faith" (Acts 15:9).

### I Answer That
Impurity results from mixture with baser things. The rational creature becomes impure by subjecting itself to transient, corporeal creatures through loving them. Purification occurs through contrary movement—tending toward God. Faith initiates this movement, as "he that cometh to God must believe that He is" (Hebrews 11:6). Faith begins heart purification; when perfected through charity, it achieves complete purification.

### Reply to Objection 1
Intellect-resident truths principle appetitive movements, since apprehended good moves the appetite.

### Reply to Objection 2
Lifeless faith excludes impurity of error—the intellect's inordinate adherence to sensible things over Divine things. Quickened by charity, faith becomes incompatible with all impurity, as "charity covereth all sins" (Proverbs 10:12).

### Reply to Objection 3
Faith's obscurity concerns natural intellect defect in our present state, not sin's impurity.
